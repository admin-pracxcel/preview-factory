# Niche Analysis — Electrician / Electrical Services

- **Niche slug:** `electrician`
- **Category:** Trades → maps to visual category **`trades`**
- **Analyst:** Preview Factory strategy
- **Date:** 2026-06-19
- **Screen result:** PASS (local service, GBP presence, owner-operated, customer LTV well above $300, reachable via Meta interest + behavioural + geo targeting)
- **Methodology note:** First niche run under the **revised methodology** (Phase 5 = category mapping & tuning). Serves as the A3 calibration analysis. Deliberately chosen as a clear-GO, direct-to-homeowner trade to contrast the marginal `bricklayer` HOLD and exercise the full decision spectrum.

> **Currency:** all figures AUD. **Uncertainty is flagged inline** — Meta audience sizes, conversion and tenure assumptions are unvalidated and must be confirmed against live data before committing budget.

## Phase 1 — Market sizing

- **Estimated business count:** ~**45,850** electrical-services businesses in Australia (IBISWorld, *Electrical Services in Australia*, 2025), **down ~3.5% YoY** — a near-term contraction tied to the residential construction slowdown, not structural decline.
- **Industry revenue:** ~**$36.1bn (2025)**, down ~3.2% YoY (IBISWorld). The dip is cyclical (construction-linked); the structural picture is a **strong long-run tailwind** (see growth).
- **Geographic distribution:** no clean per-niche ABS split available; electrical work tracks population and construction, concentrating in **Greater Sydney (NSW), Melbourne (VIC), South-East Queensland (Brisbane/Gold Coast/Sunshine Coast)**, then **Perth (WA)** and **Adelaide (SA)**. Unlike bricklaying, demand is **not** confined to new-build corridors — established suburbs generate continuous maintenance, fault, switchboard-upgrade, solar and EV-charger work. *(Triangulation only — confirm with ABS by SA4 before targeting spend.)*
- **Growth rate estimate:** **near-term flat/down, long-term strong.** Jobs and Skills Australia flags a **persistent electrician shortage** and estimates Australia needs **53,000–84,000 more electricians** for the net-zero energy transition. Electrification (solar, home batteries, EV chargers, switchboard upgrades, electrified appliances) is a durable demand driver — a genuine tailwind niche, in contrast to bricklaying's flat industry.
- **Addressable subset (Meta-reachable):** ~131,900–186,000 *electricians* are employed in Australia, but most are employees/apprentices, not business owners. Against ~45,850 businesses, the realistic reachable owner-operator pool with a findable digital footprint (FB Page / recent business admin / GBP) is a **working estimate of 15,000–25,000 nationally** — materially larger than bricklaying's 3,000–5,000, and reachable via both interest *and* behaviour (see Phase 4). *(Estimate — unverified.)*

## Phase 2 — Customer profile

- **Annual business revenue range:** sole-trader electricians average ~**$282,520** revenue (Trade Risk / upcover trade data); small crews (2–5) materially higher. Owner take-home commonly **$80k–$100k+**. Higher revenue base than bricklaying ($100k–$250k) → more headroom for a managed-tool subscription.
- **Team size:** **sole trader or small crew** dominant, with a meaningful tail of established 5–20-person contracting firms (commercial/strata work). The owner is usually on the tools but increasingly office-based as the firm grows.
- **Decision-maker:** the **owner**, frequently with a **spouse/partner or office admin** handling quoting, scheduling and "marketing." Larger firms may have an office manager.
- **Current digital sophistication:** **low-to-moderate.** Many have a basic/old site or Facebook-only presence with an unmanaged GBP. More digitally active than brickies (job-management apps like ServiceM8/Tradify are common), but the *website* is typically neglected — a strong fit for "your site doesn't match how good your business is."
- **Average customer transaction value:** wide and **high-frequency**. Domestic service calls / fault-finding **$150–$600**; switchboard upgrades, rewires, lighting, ceiling fans **$800–$5,000**; solar/battery/EV-charger installs **$2,000–$15,000+**; commercial/strata contracts recurring and larger.
- **Customer lifetime value to the business:**
  - **Homeowners:** moderate ticket but **genuinely repeat** (faults, additions, renos, solar/EV add-ons over years) → LTV often **$1,000–$5,000+** across the relationship.
  - **Builders / real estate / strata / commercial:** recurring maintenance contracts → LTV **$10k+/year**.
  Either way, far above the $300 screen threshold. **Crucially, unlike bricklaying, a large share of demand is direct-to-end-customer search**, so a consumer-facing lead-gen website captures a much bigger slice of total demand.
- **Typical marketing budget per month:** **$200–$1,500**, more planned than brickies (more owners already run Google/GBP). Some already buy SEO or run ads — a double-edged fact (warmer to the value, but some already covered).
- **Booking/inquiry cycle:** **mixed — emergency/instant-call (faults, outages, safety) AND scheduled/quoted (installs, upgrades, solar).** The emergency segment makes "more phone calls now" an immediate, visceral value frame — stronger than bricklaying's purely quote-led cycle.

## Phase 3 — Willingness to pay

**Reference points (AUD):**

- **Managed tradie website services:** ~**$99/month** (heytradie, busi.au, Tradiecard and similar AU tradie-site providers), typically no/low setup.
- **DIY builders (Wix / Squarespace):** **$30–$50/month** + many hours of owner time.
- **Agency one-off build:** **$1,500–$3,000** template-based; **$5,000+** custom.
- **Existing ad spend:** many electricians already spend **$200–$1,500/mo** on Google/GBP/lead services — a site subscription is small relative to their current marketing outlay.
- **Value of one job:** a single additional domestic install ($800–$5,000) or solar/EV job ($2k–$15k) returns **many years** of any tier below. ROI is not the constraint; perceived value is — but electricians' direct-search demand makes the link "site → calls → jobs" far more believable than for builder-referral-driven brickies.

**Recommended tiers (AUD/month):**

| Tier | Price | Justification |
|---|---|---|
| **Entry** | **$49** | Undercuts the ~$99 managed benchmark by ~50% while signalling "managed-grade, not a toy." Set above bricklaying's $39 because electricians have a **higher revenue base (~$282k vs $100–250k), direct-search demand, and higher existing marketing spend** — $49 is still a clear no-brainer (one domestic job/year returns ~15×+ the annual cost) but captures more of a higher willingness-to-pay. |
| **Growth** | **$99** | Matches the managed-competitor headline price while delivering **more** (programmatic local service-area pages + review acquisition + emergency-call landing pages). The "same price, more value" anchor is deliberate. Targets owners actively chasing direct residential work. |
| **Scale** | **$199** | Adds Google/Meta ads management + **LSA management** (electricians are LSA-eligible — see Phase 6) + quote/booking tools. Justified for crews pursuing solar/EV/commercial volume where a single managed lead is worth hundreds in job value. More electricians will reach this tier than brickies would, given direct-demand economics. |

*Flagged uncertainty: tier prices are reasoned against market anchors, not tested. Validate with a live price test before locking.*

## Phase 4 — Meta ads targeting viability

Verified against `strategy/research/meta-job-titles.md` (Category 1 — Trades).

- **Job-title / interest availability:** **usable, unlike bricklaying.** "Electrician / Electrical contractor" is a **🟢 reliable topic interest** — but, per the master file, it is **consumer-contaminated** (mixes homeowners needing an electrician with practitioners) and the *job title* "Electrician" is only 🟡. So the interest is a **narrowing/lookalike-seed signal, not the spine.**
- **Behavioural targeting (the spine):** 🟢 reliable platform behaviours —
  - **Small business owners** (primary)
  - **New active business admins (<12 / <24 months)** — "just started, needs a site"
  - **Facebook Page admins** — proxy for "runs a business with a FB presence, weak/no website" = our ICP
- **Recommended stack:** *Small business owners* (or *New active business admins*) **+ "Electrician/Electrical contractor" interest as a narrowing layer + tight suburb/postcode geo**, graduating to **AU 1–3% lookalikes** from converters once seed volume exists. The presence of a usable interest is a real advantage over bricklaying (which had no interest spine at all).
- **Geographic targeting:** broad metro + regional **suburb/postcode clusters** — demand is everywhere there are homes, not just new estates. Suburb-level where audience size allows.
- **Estimated CPM:** AU baseline ~**$11** (2026 range ~$10.68–$14.34; AU runs ~21% below global). *(Reused from the 2026 AU Meta benchmark cited for bricklaying; re-verify.)*
- **Estimated CTR / CPL:** trades/home-services local lead creative reaches **~$8–$12 CPL** at the efficient end; broader AU range **$20–$80**. Because electrician benefits from **both interest + behaviour layering and an emergency-demand creative hook**, expect the **mid-to-efficient** end rather than bricklaying's mid-to-high.
- **Creative angle:** *"Get more local electrical jobs in [suburb] — see your new website free in 3 hours,"* with an emergency/safety hook for the fault-call segment.

**Verdict:** `meta_targeting_viable: true` (interest + behaviour). Stronger targeting profile than most trades — a usable 🟢 interest *plus* the standard behavioural spine *plus* a credible direct-demand creative hook → lower expected CAC than bricklaying.

## Phase 5 — Category mapping & tuning

**Category mapping:** **`trades`.** Electrical services is a licensed building/construction trade serving homeowners and commercial clients with installation, repair and maintenance — the defining profile of the `trades` visual category (alongside plumbing, HVAC, carpentry, etc.). No new category required; **no `category_required: new` flag.**

**Niche-specific tuning within the `trades` category:**

- **Colour emphasis:** within the trades palette, lean **safety/energy-forward** — a confident electric blue or deep navy primary with a high-visibility **amber/yellow accent** (the universal electrical-safety cue), against a strong neutral base. Distinct from bricklaying's earthen/masonry tones while staying inside the shared trades palette.
- **Critical sections (prominence order):**
  1. **Emergency / 24-7 call-out banner** with click-to-call — the highest-converting element for the fault-call segment; must be above the fold.
  2. **Licence number displayed prominently** (legally expected for electrical work; a primary trust driver).
  3. **Services grid** structured for the electrification mix: fault-finding, switchboard upgrades, rewiring, lighting, **solar & battery, EV charger installation**, ceiling fans/appliances, commercial/strata.
  4. **Service-area pages** (programmatic local SEO across metro/regional suburbs — high search demand here, see Phase 6).
  5. **Reviews / Google rating** block (trust + conversion).
  6. **Quote / booking CTA** + call tracking.
- **Trust signal priorities:** **electrical licence/registration number** (top priority — display it), **public liability insurance**, **Master Electricians Australia / NECA membership** badges, manufacturer accreditations (e.g. solar/CEC-accredited installer), years of experience, warranty/guarantee.
- **Content tone:** **direct, safety-authoritative, reassuring** — "licensed, insured, on time." Slightly more authoritative/compliance-forward than bricklaying's plain-spoken craftsmanship voice, reflecting the safety-critical nature of the trade.
- **Image style cues (stock fallback):** clean modern switchboards, an electrician in PPE on a domestic call, solar panels / battery units, an EV charger on a home wall, downlight/lighting installs. Bright, safe, professional — avoid dark or hazard-implying imagery.
- **Schema.org subtype:** `Electrician` (subtype of `LocalBusiness` / `HomeAndConstructionBusiness`), with `Service` and `areaServed` markup on service-area pages.

**Build dependency:** the `trades` category currently exists only as a **single-page** component (`/templates/tradies/page.tsx`, ~798 lines) — there is **no multi-page site system** yet (no `/templates/categories/`, no `SiteProps`). Electrician needs the same **multi-page trades site system** every niche now requires. **Flag: trades category must be expanded to the multi-page architecture (Phase C) before this niche can be generated as specified.** This is a category-level dependency, not an electrician-specific build.

## Phase 6 — Niche-specific upsells

- **SEO module:** **high** (vs bricklaying's medium). "Electrician near me / [suburb] electrician / emergency electrician" carries **strong, continuous local search volume** with direct purchase intent — programmatic service-area pages have real upside here. This is a headline upsell.
- **Local Services Ads (LSA):** **HIGH — and confirmed eligible.** Electricians are an **eligible LSA vertical in the Home Services category** in Australia (Google Guaranteed; pay-per-lead, screening/licence/insurance checks required). This is a **major differentiator vs bricklaying** (whose LSA eligibility was unconfirmed). LSA management is a strong Scale-tier upsell and a credible "we get you Google-Guaranteed leads" hook. *(Verify the customer's specific eligibility at signup — LSA requires passing Google's licence/insurance screening.)*
- **Review acquisition:** **high.** Electrical is trust- and safety-driven; Google rating directly lifts both organic conversion and LSA ranking. Strong fit.
- **Niche-specific upsells:**
  - **Online booking / job request** (fits the high-frequency, mixed emergency+scheduled cycle).
  - **Emergency-call routing / after-hours capture** (monetises the fault-call segment).
  - **Solar / EV-charger landing pages** (captures the electrification growth wedge — high-ticket).
  - **Quote calculator** for common jobs (switchboard upgrade, downlights, EV charger).

## Phase 7 — Unit economics

*All inputs flagged as estimates; validate against live cohort data.*

**CAC** = cost per free-preview signup ÷ free→paid conversion. Interest + behaviour targeting and an emergency-demand creative hook → signup CPL toward the **mid/efficient** AU range (better than bricklaying's behaviour-only mid/high).

| Scenario | Signup CPL | Free→paid conv. | **CAC** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bear | $35 | 6% | **~$583** |
| Base | $22 | 10% | **~$220** |
| Bull | $12 | 15% | **~$80** |

**LTV** = entry $49/mo × projected tenure (blended up for upgrades to growth/scale, which more electricians reach than brickies given direct-demand economics).

| Scenario | Tenure | Notes | **LTV** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bear | 9 mo | high churn, price-sensitive sole trader | **~$441** |
| Base | 18 mo | entry→partial upgrade, blended ARPU ~$55 | **~$990** |
| Bull | 30+ mo | upgrades to growth/scale (ads + LSA) | **~$2,500+** |

**LTV:CAC** — Bear **~0.8:1 (fails)** · Base **~4.5:1 (clears the 3:1 target)** · Bull **~30:1.**

**Payback period:** base CAC $220 ÷ ~$55 blended ARPU ≈ **4.0 months** (vs bricklaying's 6.4).

**Read:** base-case economics are **strong and clear the 3:1 bar**, driven by (a) interest + behaviour targeting → lower CAC, (b) direct-to-homeowner + emergency demand → a lead-gen site captures real, attributable jobs, (c) higher willingness-to-pay and upgrade rate (LSA/ads tiers), and (d) a structural electrification tailwind. The bear case still fails on high churn, so onboarding/activation and demonstrated call volume are the retention levers to watch.

## Phase 8 — Competitive landscape

- **Niche/tradie-specific builders:** crowded AU field — **heytradie, busi.au, Tradiecard, Websites Factory, ServiceScale, BuiltByJack** — typically **~$99/month managed**, template-based, generic across trades. Electrician is one of their core verticals, so the competition is sharper here than for bricklaying.
- **Specialist electrician marketers:** several AU agencies (e.g. Tradies Get Online, Uprise Digital) run **Google Ads / LSA management for electricians** at agency retainers — these compete for the *ads/LSA* upsell more than the *site* itself, and validate that the LSA/ads upsell has a real market.
- **Generic builders:** **Wix / Squarespace** ($30–$50/mo, DIY); **Durable** (AI builder) entering AU SMB.
- **Local agencies:** numerous AU agencies doing **$1,500–$5,000+** one-off electrician sites + monthly SEO.
- **Pricing benchmark:** managed ~$99/mo to beat; DIY $30–50/mo floor; specialist ads/LSA management commands much higher retainers (justifies our Scale tier).
- **Differentiating angle for Preview Factory:** **AI-generated, electrician-true preview live in ~3 hours, free trial, with programmatic local-SEO service-area pages, emergency-call capture, LSA-ready setup and call tracking**, framed as *"more local electrical jobs"* — at **$49 entry, half the $99 managed pack**, with an upgrade path into managed LSA/ads that competes with the agency retainers at self-serve pricing. Differentiation = *speed-to-see-it* + *local-SEO/LSA depth* + *price*.

## Phase 9 — Decision

**Decision: GO.**

Electrician is a flagship trade for v1. It clears the screen comfortably and, unlike bricklaying, clears the **3:1 LTV:CAC bar in the base case (~4.5:1, ~4-month payback)**. Three structural strengths drive this: **direct-to-end-customer demand** (a lead-gen site captures attributable jobs, including high-value emergency calls), **usable Meta targeting** (a 🟢 interest *plus* the behavioural spine, lowering CAC), and a **durable electrification/net-zero tailwind** (solar, batteries, EV chargers, electrification) against a persistent labour shortage. Willingness-to-pay is higher (revenue base ~$282k, existing marketing spend), supporting a $49 entry and a meaningful upgrade path — including **confirmed LSA eligibility**, a differentiated Scale-tier upsell unavailable to bricklaying. The main risk is churn among price-sensitive sole traders (bear case fails), making activation and demonstrated call volume the key retention levers. The only blocker is a **category-level build dependency**: the `trades` template must be expanded from single-page to the multi-page site system before this niche can be generated as specified.

## Phase 10 — Implementation priority

- **Priority tier:** **Tier 1** (launch in first 90 days) — flagship trade, strong economics, high search/LSA demand.
- **Build requirements:** **category-level, not electrician-specific.** Expand the `trades` category from the current single-page `/templates/tradies/page.tsx` to the **multi-page site system** (home + services + service-detail + location/service-area + FAQ + about) under the new `SiteProps` schema (Phase C / Phase D). Electrician-specific tuning (per Phase 5) is then applied at generation time via the niche system prompt — emergency banner, licence-number prominence, electrification services grid, blue/amber emphasis, `Electrician` schema.
- **Compliance considerations:** standard Meta ad policy; **no Special Ad Category triggers** (B2B website offer). Ensure **licence numbers, insurance and accreditation claims on generated sites are owner-supplied, not fabricated** (safety-critical, regulated trade). LSA upsell requires the customer to pass Google's screening (licence/insurance) — position as "we set you up to qualify," not "guaranteed approval."
