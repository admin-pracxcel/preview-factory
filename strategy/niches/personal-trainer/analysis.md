# Niche Analysis — Personal Training / Fitness Studio

- **Niche slug:** `personal-trainer`
- **Category:** Fitness → maps to visual category **`fitness-wellness`**
- **Analyst:** Preview Factory strategy
- **Date:** 2026-06-19
- **Screen result:** PASS with caveats (local service, GBP/social presence, owner-operated, Meta-native visual vertical) — but **two material risks**: thin sole-trader margins and **audience inversion** (Meta "fitness" interests overwhelmingly reach *clients*, not *studio owners*). Customer LTV clears $300 for studios/membership models; marginal for pure session-based sole-trader PTs.
- **Methodology note:** Run under the revised methodology (Phase 5 = category mapping & tuning). Covers a heterogeneous niche spanning three sub-types — **sole-trader PTs**, **small-group studios / bootcamps**, and **reformer Pilates studios** — which differ sharply in margin, LTV and willingness-to-pay. Tuning and economics are stated per sub-type where it matters.

> **Currency:** all figures AUD. **Uncertainty is flagged inline** — Meta audience sizes, conversion and tenure assumptions are unvalidated and must be confirmed against live data before committing budget.

## Phase 1 — Market sizing

- **Estimated business count:** the registered/structured end is ~**8,078 gym & fitness-centre businesses** (IBISWorld, *Gym & Fitness Centre Operation in Australia*, 2025). This **undercounts the true niche badly** — it excludes the large population of sole-trader PTs and many micro-studios operating as individuals. **AUSactive** (formerly Fitness Australia) lists **~11,000+ registered exercise professionals**, and **Jobs and Skills Australia / industry data put ~32,000 personal trainers + ~12,400 group-fitness instructors (~45,200 fitness workers, 2023)** in the workforce. Triangulating: the **owner-operator / micro-business pool** (sole-trader PTs + small studios + reformer Pilates studios with their own brand and GBP) is a **working estimate of 20,000–35,000 nationally** — much larger than the 8,078 IBISWorld business count because most of the niche is sole traders. *(Triangulation only — three different denominators; confirm before spend.)*
- **Industry revenue:** gym & fitness centres ~**$3.7bn (2025)**, down ~**1.3% YoY** (IBISWorld) — a mild cyclical contraction as budget-gym churn and cost-of-living pressure bite. The **Australian personal-training segment** is separately estimated at ~**$433m**. **Reformer Pilates / boutique studios are the growth pocket** (see growth).
- **Geographic distribution:** no clean per-niche ABS split; fitness demand tracks population and disposable income, concentrating in **Greater Sydney (NSW), Melbourne (VIC), South-East Queensland (Brisbane/Gold Coast/Sunshine Coast)**, then **Perth (WA)**, **Adelaide (SA)**, **Canberra (ACT)** and regional centres. Boutique reformer Pilates skews to **affluent metro suburbs** (Canberra has the highest average reformer pricing ~$82/wk, then Sydney ~$77, Melbourne ~$75). *(Triangulation only — confirm with ABS by SA4 before targeting spend.)*
- **Growth rate estimate:** **mixed and bifurcated.** Traditional/budget gyms are **flat-to-declining** (-1.3% industry YoY). But **boutique studios — especially reformer Pilates — are a genuine boom**: the AU Pilates & yoga studios market is growing at a **~8.4% CAGR (2025–2033)**, with new franchise entrants (e.g. JetSet Pilates launching in Melbourne early 2025). Reformer Pilates is described as among the most expensive workouts in Australia, supporting **premium pricing and longer client lifecycles** (older adults, pre/postnatal, rehab). Net: **the studio/reformer sub-type is a tailwind; the sole-trader PT sub-type is flat and crowded.**
- **Addressable subset (Meta-reachable):** of the 20,000–35,000 owner pool, the realistic reachable set with a findable digital footprint (FB/IG Page, recent business admin, GBP) is large — fitness operators are unusually **social/Meta-native**. Working estimate **15,000–25,000 nationally**. **The constraint is not reach but precision**: Meta cannot cleanly separate the *owner* from the *millions of fitness-interested consumers* (see Phase 4). *(Estimate — unverified.)*

## Phase 2 — Customer profile

The niche splits three ways; profile each:

- **Annual business revenue range:**
  - **Sole-trader PT:** highly variable — owner take-home commonly **$50k–$110k**; top specialists combining 1:1 + group + online coaching exceed **$150k–$200k**. But **thin margins**: many rent floor space in a host gym (~$300–$400/week) or pay 50% revenue split, leaving little discretionary marketing budget.
  - **Small-group studio / bootcamp:** **$150k–$500k+** revenue; better margins than a host-gym PT, real fixed costs (lease, equipment).
  - **Reformer Pilates studio:** **$300k–$1m+** for an established multi-reformer studio; premium per-class pricing and membership recurring revenue → **the strongest economics in the niche.**
- **Team size:** **sole trader** (PT) → **owner + a few contract instructors** (small studio) → **owner-operator + employed/contract instructor roster** (reformer studio).
- **Decision-maker:** the **owner** — very often the founding PT/instructor themselves, frequently a **young, social-media-fluent operator**, sometimes with a **spouse/partner** handling admin/bookings. Higher self-promotion fluency than trades, lower than a marketing agency.
- **Current digital sophistication:** **moderate-to-high on social, low-to-moderate on web.** Almost all have an active **Instagram/Facebook** presence; many run on **Linktree + a booking tool (Mindbody/Glofox/ClassPass)** with **no real website** or a thin one. This is the wedge: *"your Instagram is great, your website doesn't exist / looks nothing like your brand."* Note: more brand-conscious than trades → position on **brand quality and bookings**, not "you have no online presence."
- **Average customer transaction value:**
  - **1:1 PT session:** ~**$60–$120/hr** (national avg ~$75–$85); mobile PTs $80–$130.
  - **Group / small-group:** **$15–$40 per head**.
  - **Reformer Pilates class:** **$25–$50 per class**; class packs and memberships standard.
  - **Memberships:** boutique studios **$150–$300/month unlimited**; reformer passes average **$70–$82/week** in metro.
- **Customer lifetime value to the business:** **the key variable, and it is sub-type dependent.**
  - **Sole-trader 1:1 PT:** clients often train 1–3×/week for **6–18 months** → LTV commonly **$2,000–$8,000+** per client — *high per client, but the business itself is fragile/low-margin.*
  - **Studio/reformer membership:** recurring **$150–$300/mo × 6–24 month tenure** → member LTV **$1,500–$6,000+**, and the **studio aggregates dozens-to-hundreds of members** → strong, recurring business-level LTV. **Well above the $300 screen threshold.**
  - **Caveat:** the *end-customer* LTV is high, but what matters for *us* is the **owner's** willingness and ability to pay for a website — gated by **margin**, which is thin for sole traders and healthy for studios.
- **Typical marketing budget per month:** **$0–$300 for sole-trader PTs** (many spend $0 and rely on word-of-mouth + Instagram); **$300–$2,000 for studios** (boutique/reformer studios actively run Meta ads for trials and memberships — they are already buyers of paid acquisition).
- **Booking/inquiry cycle:** **consultation/trial-led and recurring.** Almost always a **free trial / intro offer / first-class-free** funnel → conversion to ongoing sessions or membership. **Not emergency.** The "more local calls" outcome frame must be re-cast as **"more trial bookings / more members"** — bookings, not phone calls, are the currency.

## Phase 3 — Willingness to pay

**Reference points (AUD):**

- **Managed-SMB website benchmark:** ~**$99/month** (the cross-niche managed-site anchor used across this project).
- **DIY builders (Wix / Squarespace):** **$30–$50/month** + many owner-hours — many PTs/studios currently sit here or on Linktree.
- **Fitness booking/management software (the dominant anchor for this niche):** **Mindbody from ~A$89/mo entry**, Starter ~A$139, scaling to **$469–$700/mo** with branded app/SMS/reporting; **Glofox quote-only, ~$100–$600+/mo**; 24-month contracts common. **Studios already pay $89–$600/mo for software** — a website subscription sits comfortably inside this established budget line.
- **Agency one-off build:** **$1,500–$3,000** template-based; **$5,000+** custom — common for established reformer studios that want a branded site.
- **Value of one client/member:** a single new 1:1 client ($2,000–$8,000 LTV) or one retained membership ($1,500–$6,000 LTV) returns **many years** of any tier below. ROI is not the constraint; **margin and perceived need** are — sole-trader PTs question whether they need a website at all when Instagram + a booking link "works."

**Recommended tiers (AUD/month):**

| Tier | Price | Justification |
|---|---|---|
| **Entry** | **$39** | Set **below** the ~$99 managed benchmark and **below the $89 Mindbody floor** to clear the no-brainer bar for **margin-thin sole-trader PTs** — the segment most likely to balk at "do I even need a website?" $39 is one extra 1:1 session every two months; trivially justified, and undercuts the booking-tool anchor they already grudgingly pay. Lower than electrician's $49 because PT sole-trader margins are thinner and need-perception is weaker (Instagram substitutes). |
| **Growth** | **$89** | Matches the **Mindbody entry price** the studio already accepts as "normal fitness software spend," while delivering a full branded multi-page site + class timetable/booking embed + trial-offer landing pages + local SEO. The deliberate anchor: *"same as your booking software, but it's your actual website + bookings funnel."* Targets **small-group and reformer studios** actively chasing trials/members. |
| **Scale** | **$179** | Adds **Meta ads management** (studios already buy paid trials — this is the live upsell), review acquisition, membership/trial-funnel optimisation and booking integration. Justified for **reformer/boutique studios** where one retained member is worth $1,500–$6,000 and acquisition is already a budgeted line at $300–$2,000/mo. Slightly below electrician's $199 because there is no LSA upsell in this vertical (fitness is not LSA-eligible — see Phase 6). |

*Flagged uncertainty: tier prices are reasoned against market anchors (Mindbody/Glofox, managed-SMB $99, agency builds), not tested. The $39 entry is deliberately defensive against sole-trader price sensitivity; validate with a live price test, and consider a studio-only $59 entry if sole-trader conversion proves weak.*

## Phase 4 — Meta ads targeting viability

Verified against `strategy/research/meta-job-titles.md` (Category 8 — Fitness).

- **Job-title / interest availability:** the interests are **🟢 large and Meta-native** — "Personal trainer / Personal training," "Gym / Fitness centre," "Pilates / Reformer Pilates," "Yoga," "CrossFit" are all well-populated AU topic interests, and **"Personal trainer" as a job title is more owner-aligned than most occupations (🟡)**. **BUT the master file's central warning applies hardest here: these interests are *gigantic and consumer-dominated*.** "Fitness," "Gym," "Yoga," "Pilates" reach **millions of Australians who *do* fitness**, not the few thousand who *own a studio*. This is the **audience-inversion risk** flagged in the brief — without a hard behavioural layer, the ad spend pours onto consumers (would-be clients), not owners.
- **Behavioural targeting (the spine — mandatory here):** 🟢 reliable platform behaviours —
  - **Small business owners** (primary)
  - **New active business admins (<12 / <24 months)** — "just opened a studio, needs a site" (strong fit given the reformer-studio opening boom)
  - **Facebook Page admins** — proxy for "runs a business with a social presence" = our ICP
- **Recommended stack:** *Small business owners* (or *New active business admins*) **+ "Personal training / Pilates / Gym" interest as a narrowing layer + tight suburb/postcode geo**, graduating to **AU 1–3% lookalikes** from converters/customer list as fast as possible — **lookalikes matter more here than in almost any other niche** because they are the only reliable way to escape consumer contamination at scale. **Creative must do the qualifying** ("Own a studio?" / "Get more trial bookings for your gym") since targeting cannot.
- **Geographic targeting:** metro + affluent-suburb **postcode clusters** (reformer/boutique skews affluent); suburb-level where audience size allows.
- **Estimated CPM:** AU baseline ~**$11** (2026; AU all-industry CPM ~$9.80, fitness runs near/below average). *(Anchor per brief; re-verify in Ads Manager.)*
- **Estimated CTR / CPL:** **fitness is a 2026 bright spot for Meta lead gen** — fitness CPLs *declined ~20%* against a rising-cost market, with reported fitness CPLs as low as **~$13.50 (April) up to ~$48 (July seasonal peak)** and high conversion rates (studios reportedly up to ~14%). **Caveat:** those benchmarks are for *consumer* fitness lead gen (clients booking trials), **not** B2B owner acquisition — our B2B-on-consumer-platform CPL will run **higher** than the headline fitness numbers because of the inversion problem. Expect **mid AU range**, improving sharply once lookalikes seed.
- **Creative angle:** *"Own a PT studio or run reformer classes? Get a website that matches your Instagram — and fills your timetable. See it free in 3 hours,"* with the **trial-booking** outcome (not "phone calls"), and **strong visual creative** (the niche is visual and Meta-native — our creative can be unusually strong here).

**Verdict:** `meta_targeting_viable: true` — but **conditionally, and with the strongest audience-inversion caveat of any niche analysed so far.** Interests are 🟢 but consumer-dominated to an extreme; the **behavioural spine + lookalikes + owner-qualifying creative are non-negotiable**, not optional. Done wrong, the budget acquires *clients for trainers* instead of *trainers as customers*. Done right (behaviour + geo + lookalike + sharp B2B creative), Meta is a strong, visual, native channel for this niche.

## Phase 5 — Category mapping & tuning

**Category mapping:** **`fitness-wellness`.** Personal training, small-group studios and reformer Pilates studios are the *defining* members of the `fitness-wellness` visual category (alongside yoga, CrossFit, martial arts, wellness/recovery studios). The niche is **brand-led, visual, conversion-to-booking** in character — distinct from the licence/safety-led `trades` category and the clinical `allied-health` category. No new category required; **no `category_required: new` flag.** (Note: this niche sits at the `fitness-wellness` / `allied-health` boundary for *clinical* Pilates / exercise physiology, but the commercial centre of gravity — boutique studios, PTs — is clearly `fitness-wellness`.)

**Niche-specific tuning within the `fitness-wellness` category:**

- **Colour emphasis:** within the fitness-wellness palette, lean **energetic-but-premium** — for **reformer/boutique Pilates** skew to **calm, premium, editorial tones** (warm neutrals, sage/clay, soft contrast, lots of whitespace — the "expensive workout" aesthetic); for **PT/bootcamp/CrossFit** skew **bolder and higher-energy** (deep charcoal/black with a vivid accent — electric green/orange). The category palette must flex across these two moods at generation time.
- **Critical sections (prominence order):**
  1. **Class timetable + online booking** (embed of Mindbody/Glofox/ClassPass, or native booking) — the single highest-converting element; bookings are the currency. Above the fold or one tap away.
  2. **Free trial / intro offer** ("first class free," "2 weeks for $39," "free intro session") — the universal fitness funnel entry; must be a prominent, repeated CTA.
  3. **Transformations / results / social proof** — before/after, member stories, Google/Instagram reviews. The persuasion engine of the niche.
  4. **Memberships & pricing** — clear class packs / membership tiers (fitness buyers expect transparent pricing).
  5. **Instagram feed / gallery** — the niche lives on visual social proof; pull the live feed in.
  6. **About / trainer bios + certifications** (humanise; the founder *is* the brand).
  7. **Service-area / suburb pages** (programmatic local SEO — "reformer pilates [suburb]," "personal trainer [suburb]").
- **Trust signal priorities:** **certifications & qualifications** (Cert III/IV in Fitness, Pilates teacher training, exercise-science degree), **AUSactive registration** (formerly Fitness Australia — the AU peak-body register; display the badge), **public liability + professional indemnity insurance**, **first aid/CPR currency**, years of experience, **Google/Instagram review counts**. Certifications and AUSactive registration are the credibility anchors equivalent to a trade licence.
- **Content tone:** **warm, motivating, community-forward** — "join the studio," "your first class is on us," outcome- and belonging-oriented. Less formal than allied-health, more aspirational than trades. Flex: **premium/editorial** for reformer Pilates; **high-energy/direct** for PT/bootcamp.
- **Image style cues (stock fallback):** bright, real studio interiors (reformers in a clean light-filled space; turf/rig for functional gyms), people *mid-movement* and smiling, small-group energy, the trainer coaching. Avoid sterile gym-equipment-only shots and over-aggressive "fitspo." Lean authentic/diverse over hyper-fit stock.
- **Schema.org subtype:** `ExerciseGym` / `HealthClub` (subtypes of `LocalBusiness` / `SportsActivityLocation`); use `SportsActivityLocation` for studios, with `Service`/`Offer` markup for class packs and `Schedule`/`Event` markup on the timetable, and `areaServed` on suburb pages. (For clinical Pilates, `MedicalBusiness` may be more accurate — choose at generation time by sub-type.)

**Build dependency — FLAGGED (build needed).** There is currently **no `fitness-wellness` template at all**, and **no `/templates/categories/` directory exists** — the only template in `/templates/` is the **single-page trades template** (`/templates/tradies/page.tsx`). The `fitness-wellness` **category must be built from scratch as a multi-page site system** (home + timetable/booking + classes/services + trial-offer landing + memberships/pricing + about/trainers + suburb/service-area + FAQ), including the **booking-embed and Instagram-feed components** this niche specifically requires. **This is a category-level build dependency — larger than electrician's (which only needed the existing trades single-page expanded). `fitness-wellness` does not exist yet and is a net-new category build.** Personal-trainer-specific tuning (per above) is then applied at generation time via the niche system prompt.

## Phase 6 — Niche-specific upsells

- **SEO module:** **medium-to-high, sub-type dependent.** "Personal trainer near me / [suburb] personal trainer / reformer pilates [suburb] / gym [suburb]" carries **real, continuous local search volume with booking intent** — programmatic suburb pages have genuine upside, especially for **studios** (fixed location → strong local-SEO logic). **Lower for mobile sole-trader PTs** who fill via Instagram and word-of-mouth and may not rank-chase. Headline upsell for studios; secondary for sole traders.
- **Local Services Ads (LSA):** **NOT eligible.** Fitness / personal training / Pilates studios are **not in Google's LSA / Local Services eligible verticals** (LSA covers home services, legal, health-professional and select categories — not gyms/PTs/studios). **No LSA upsell exists for this niche** — a real difference vs electrician, and the reason Scale tops out at $179 not $199. *(Verify against Google's current eligibility list; treat as not-eligible for v1.)*
- **Review acquisition:** **high.** Fitness is intensely trust- and social-proof-driven; Google + Instagram reviews and transformation testimonials directly lift trial conversion. Strong fit, especially bundled into Scale.
- **Niche-specific upsells:**
  - **Online class booking / timetable integration** (Mindbody/Glofox/ClassPass embed, or native) — the **single most valuable niche upsell**; bookings are the outcome.
  - **Free-trial / intro-offer funnel + lead capture** (the fitness acquisition funnel productised).
  - **Meta ads management** (studios already buy paid trials — direct upsell, and the Scale-tier engine).
  - **Membership / class-pack page + payment links** (recurring-revenue presentation).
  - **Instagram-feed and transformation-gallery management** (leans into the visual native channel).

## Phase 7 — Unit economics

*All inputs flagged as estimates; validate against live cohort data. Economics are bifurcated — sole-trader PT vs studio — so the base case is a blend skewed by which sub-type dominates the funnel.*

**CAC** = cost per free-preview signup ÷ free→paid conversion. Fitness is a favourable AU Meta vertical for *consumer* lead gen, but our **B2B owner targeting suffers audience inversion** → signup CPL runs **above** the headline fitness CPL until lookalikes seed. Visual/native creative is a partial offset.

| Scenario | Signup CPL | Free→paid conv. | **CAC** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bear | $40 | 6% | **~$667** |
| Base | $25 | 10% | **~$250** |
| Bull | $14 | 15% | **~$93** |

**LTV** = entry $39/mo × projected tenure (blended up for studio upgrades to growth/scale; dragged down by sole-trader churn). Note entry is $39 (vs electrician's $49), so LTV per month of tenure is lower.

| Scenario | Tenure | Notes | **LTV** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bear | 8 mo | high churn, sole-trader PT who decides Instagram is enough | **~$312** |
| Base | 16 mo | entry→partial upgrade; blended ARPU ~$50 (studios pull blend up) | **~$800** |
| Bull | 28+ mo | studio upgrades to growth/scale (ads + booking + reviews) | **~$1,900+** |

**LTV:CAC** — Bear **~0.47:1 (fails badly)** · Base **~3.2:1 (clears the 3:1 target, narrowly)** · Bull **~20:1.**

**Payback period:** base CAC $250 ÷ ~$50 blended ARPU ≈ **5.0 months** (vs electrician's 4.0).

**Read:** base-case economics **clear the 3:1 bar, but only narrowly (~3.2:1)** and with a **worse bear case than electrician** — the bear scenario fails hard because (a) audience inversion inflates CAC and (b) margin-thin sole-trader PTs churn fast when they conclude Instagram + a booking link is "enough." **The economics are carried by the studio / reformer sub-type** (recurring membership LTV, existing ads budget, upgrade propensity, the growth tailwind), **not** by sole-trader PTs. The strategic implication is sharp: **bias acquisition and creative toward studios/reformer operators**, treat sole-trader PTs as a lower-priority/secondary segment, and **lean on lookalikes early** to beat the inversion problem. Activation = getting a live, on-brand site embedding their booking timetable; retention = demonstrated trial bookings.

## Phase 8 — Competitive landscape

- **Fitness-specific platforms (the dominant incumbents):** **Mindbody, Glofox, ClassPass, PushPress, Gymdesk** etc. — but these are **booking/management platforms**, not website builders. They give studios a generic booking page and a basic embeddable widget, **not a branded marketing website** — which is precisely the gap Preview Factory fills (and we *integrate* with them rather than compete head-on). Studios already pay $89–$600/mo here, normalising the spend.
- **Fitness-specific web/marketing services:** several AU agencies build PT/gym websites and run **Meta ads for studio trials** at agency retainers (the Scale-tier competitor). Validates that paid-trial acquisition is a real, budgeted market.
- **Generic builders:** **Wix / Squarespace** ($30–$50/mo, DIY — many studios are here or on **Linktree**); **Durable** (AI builder) entering AU SMB.
- **Local agencies:** numerous AU agencies doing **$1,500–$5,000+** one-off studio sites + monthly SEO/ads, especially for reformer/boutique studios investing in brand.
- **Pricing benchmark:** booking software $89–$600/mo (the anchor); managed-SMB ~$99/mo; DIY $30–50/mo floor; agency builds $1,500–$5,000+; ads management at retainer.
- **Differentiating angle for Preview Factory:** **an AI-generated, on-brand studio website live in ~3 hours, free trial, that matches the operator's Instagram aesthetic, embeds their existing booking timetable, foregrounds the free-trial funnel, and runs programmatic local SEO for "reformer pilates / personal trainer [suburb]"** — framed as *"more trial bookings and members,"* at **$39 entry**. We are **not** another booking tool and **not** a $1,500 agency build — we are the **branded marketing-site + bookings funnel layer on top of the booking tool they already pay for**, at self-serve pricing. Differentiation = *speed-to-see-it* + *brand-match to their socials* + *booking-funnel integration* + *price*.

## Phase 9 — Decision

**Decision: GO (studio-led), with conditions.**

Personal training / fitness studio is a **GO for v1, but a more conditional one than electrician.** It clears the screen and **clears the 3:1 LTV:CAC bar in the base case (~3.2:1) — but only narrowly, with a bear case that fails badly (~0.5:1)**. The GO rests almost entirely on the **studio and reformer-Pilates sub-type**: recurring membership LTV, an existing and budgeted ads spend, real upgrade propensity, and a genuine **~8.4% CAGR boutique-studio growth tailwind**. The niche is **highly visual and Meta-native**, so our creative can be unusually strong. Two risks must be actively managed: **(1) audience inversion** — Meta's "fitness/gym/pilates" interests overwhelmingly reach *clients*, not *owners*, so the behavioural spine + early lookalikes + sharp owner-qualifying B2B creative are mandatory, not optional; and **(2) sole-trader PT fragility** — thin margins and "Instagram is enough" need-perception drive the failing bear case, so sole-trader PTs should be treated as a *secondary* segment behind studios. Pricing is set defensively low at **$39 entry** to clear the sole-trader no-brainer bar, with **$89 growth** anchored to the Mindbody price studios already accept and **$179 scale** (no LSA upsell, unlike electrician). The decisive blocker is a **net-new category build**: unlike electrician (which only needed the existing trades template expanded), **`fitness-wellness` does not exist at all** — there is no `/templates/categories/` and no fitness template — so the **entire `fitness-wellness` multi-page category, including booking-embed and Instagram-feed components, must be built before this niche can ship.**

## Phase 10 — Implementation priority

- **Priority tier:** **Tier 1** (launch in first 90 days) — strong visual/Meta fit, a real growth tailwind in the reformer/boutique sub-type, and economics that clear the bar in the base case. *Tier-1 conditional on the `fitness-wellness` category build landing and on acquisition being biased toward studios over sole-trader PTs.* If the category build slips, this naturally drops to **early Tier 2** behind the trades niches whose category is closer to ready.
- **Build requirements:** **category-level and net-new.** Build the **`fitness-wellness` category as a multi-page site system from scratch** (home + class timetable/booking + classes/services + trial-offer landing + memberships/pricing + about/trainers + suburb/service-area + FAQ) under the `SiteProps` schema, **including niche-critical components: booking-tool embed (Mindbody/Glofox/ClassPass) and live Instagram feed.** Personal-trainer tuning (Phase 5) — timetable/trial-offer prominence, transformation gallery, AUSactive trust badge, dual premium/high-energy palette mode, `ExerciseGym`/`SportsActivityLocation` schema — applied at generation time via the niche system prompt.
- **Compliance considerations:** standard Meta ad policy (fitness creative is generally fine; **avoid body-shaming / unrealistic-outcome claims**, which can trip Meta's health-and-wellness ad policy, and avoid "guaranteed results"). Ensure **certifications, AUSactive registration, insurance and testimonial/transformation claims on generated sites are owner-supplied and genuine, not fabricated.** No Special Ad Category triggers (B2B website offer). **No LSA** — do not position an LSA upsell for this niche.
