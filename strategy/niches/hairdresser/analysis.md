## Phase 1 — Market sizing

- **Estimated business count:** IBISWorld's reporting unit is **"Hairdressing and Beauty Services"** at **~39,543 businesses (2025), up ~8.4% YoY** (IBISWorld). This bundles hair salons, barbers and beauty/skin businesses into one industry code, so it **overstates hairdressing-only**. Triangulating: hair salons + barbershops are generally estimated at **~55–65%** of this combined population, giving a working estimate of **~22,000–26,000 hairdressing/hair-salon businesses** in Australia. *(Triangulation only — the headline 39,543 is the directly reported figure for the combined industry; the hair-only split is an estimate and must be confirmed before targeting spend.)*
- **Industry revenue:** the combined Hairdressing & Beauty Services industry is **~$12.4bn (2025-26)**, with revenue forecast to **dip ~0.3% in 2025-26** on household-budget pressure cutting premium treatments (IBISWorld). *(Note: a second IBISWorld series cites a narrower ~$7.5bn definition with a 5-yr decline — definitional differences between the broad and narrow industry codes; treat the ~$12.4bn broad figure as the headline and flag the discrepancy.)* Hairdressing-only revenue is therefore on the order of **$7–8bn**, the largest slice of the category.
- **Geographic distribution:** no clean per-niche ABS split; hairdressing tracks population density almost exactly (every suburb has salons), concentrating in **Greater Sydney (NSW), Melbourne (VIC), South-East Queensland (Brisbane/Gold Coast/Sunshine Coast)**, then **Perth (WA)**, **Adelaide (SA)**, and a long, dense regional/suburban tail. Unlike trades, hairdressing demand is **uniformly distributed** — there is no "new-build corridor" dependency; foot-traffic suburbs are the unit of demand. *(Triangulation only — confirm with ABS by SA4 before targeting spend.)*
- **Growth rate estimate:** **flat-to-slightly-down near term, stable long term.** The category is mature and non-discretionary at the maintenance end (cuts, colour upkeep), but premium/treatment spend is cyclically soft under current household budget pressure. Business *count* is rising (+8.4% YoY) — partly chair-rental and micro-salon formation — even as per-business revenue softens. Net: a **large, stable, fragmented market**, not a tailwind growth story like electrification.
- **Addressable subset (Meta-reachable):** Jobs and Skills Australia's hairdressers occupation pool is large (tens of thousands employed), but most are employees, apprentices or chair renters, not business owners. Against ~22,000–26,000 hair businesses, the realistic reachable **owner-operator** pool with a findable digital footprint (FB Page / Instagram business / recent business admin / GBP) is a **working estimate of 12,000–18,000 nationally** — and, critically, this niche's owners are **unusually digitally active and visual** (Instagram-native), so a *higher share* of the owner pool is genuinely Meta-reachable than in any trade. *(Estimate — unverified.)*

## Phase 2 — Customer profile

- **Annual business revenue range:** wide. Single-chair / micro-salons and chair-rent operators **~$80k–$200k**; established small salons (3–8 chairs) **~$300k–$700k**; larger multi-station salons **$1m+**. **Mid-margin** — labour-intensive with significant wage, rent and product costs; net margins are typically **thinner than trades**, and owners are **price-sensitive** on overheads. *(Triangulation from IBISWorld per-business revenue and salon-cost benchmarks.)*
- **Team size:** **sole operator / chair-renter through small team (3–10 stylists)** dominant, with a tail of larger salons. The owner is almost always **on the floor cutting** while also running the books.
- **Decision-maker:** the **owner** (very often owner-operator), frequently the founder-stylist; in small salons a **spouse/partner or front-of-house manager** handles bookings and "the socials." Highly personal, brand-as-self businesses.
- **Current digital sophistication:** **distinctive profile — high on social, low on website.** Most salons have an **active, well-curated Instagram** (portfolio of work) and a GBP, but **no real website or an outdated one**, and rely on **Instagram DMs + a booking link (Fresha/Timely/Square)** rather than an owned site. This is the inverse of trades: they are *already visual marketers* but their **owned web presence is the weak point**. The pitch is "your Instagram is beautiful — your website should match, and it should take bookings."
- **Average customer transaction value:** **~$80–$250 per visit** typically; a women's cut-and-colour commonly **$150–$300+**, men's/barber **$30–$60**, premium colour correction/extensions **$300–$800+**. *(Industry benchmark ~$150 average service ticket plus retail.)*
- **Customer lifetime value to the business:** **genuinely high via frequency and loyalty.** Regulars rebook every **4–8 weeks**; a retained colour client at ~$200 every 6 weeks is **~$1,500–$1,700/year**, and salon clients commonly stay **multiple years** → relationship LTV often **$3,000–$8,000+**. Loyalty/retention is the entire economic engine of a salon (industry data: retention drives the bulk of CLV uplift). Far above the $300 screen threshold, and a *new regular* is worth a large multiple of any subscription tier.
- **Typical marketing budget per month:** **~$100–$800.** Lower planned cash budget than trades, but heavy *time* investment in Instagram. Some run boosted-post Meta ads already; few do structured SEO. Price-sensitive on fixed monthly commitments.
- **Booking/inquiry cycle:** **scheduled and recurring, booking-led** (not emergency). The dominant action is **"book an appointment online,"** ideally a real-time calendar. This makes **online booking the single most important conversion element** of the site — more so than the click-to-call that dominates trades.

## Phase 3 — Willingness to pay

**Reference points (AUD):**

- **Managed-SMB website benchmark:** ~**$99/month** is the prevailing managed-website price point across AU SMB providers (the "managed-grade" anchor reused across this project).
- **DIY builders (Wix / Squarespace):** **$30–$50/month** + many hours of owner time; both heavily marketed to salons.
- **Agency one-off salon build:** template-based salon sites **~$500–$1,000**; customised **$1,000–$4,000**; agency "premium" salon builds **$5,000–$15,000**.
- **Salon booking tools as the *real* pricing anchor:** salons already pay for booking software — **Timely AUD ~$42–$79 per staff member/month** (a 5-person salon can exceed **$300/mo**); **Fresha** is low-headline (~USD $19.95/mo individual) **but takes a ~20% commission on new marketplace clients + payment fees**. Salons are *used to* paying a recurring software fee tied to bookings — this is the spend our subscription sits beside, and the framing "owned bookings, no per-client commission" is a wedge against Fresha specifically.
- **Value of one client:** a single new *regular* colour client is worth **~$1,500/year** and **$3,000–$8,000** lifetime — one retained new client per year returns many multiples of any tier below.

**Recommended tiers (AUD/month):**

| Tier | Price | Justification |
|---|---|---|
| **Entry** | **$39** | Set **below** the trades $49 entry because salons are **mid-margin and price-sensitive on fixed monthly overheads**, and they already carry a booking-software line item (Timely/Fresha). $39 clears the no-brainer bar (one retained client returns ~35×+ the annual cost) and undercuts the $99 managed benchmark by ~60% while still signalling "managed-grade, not a free toy." Visual quality — not price — is the purchase driver here, so the entry tier leads on *look* and *bookings*, not cheapness. |
| **Growth** | **$89** | Sits just under the $99 managed headline while delivering **more**: integrated **online-booking embed**, programmatic local service-area pages (e.g. "balayage [suburb]", "men's haircut [suburb]"), Instagram-feed integration, and review acquisition. Targets owners actively chasing new local clients beyond their Instagram following. The "near-$99, more value" anchor is deliberate. |
| **Scale** | **$169** | Adds Meta/Google ads management (this audience lives on Instagram — managed paid social is a natural upsell), advanced booking + rebooking automation, and review/loyalty tooling. Justified for multi-chair salons where a single retained client is worth $1,500+/yr; priced under the trades $199 Scale because the upsell ceiling (no LSA — see Phase 6) and margin profile are lower. |

*Flagged uncertainty: tier prices are reasoned against market anchors (managed $99, DIY $30–50, Timely $42–79/staff, agency $500–15k), not tested. The lower entry vs trades reflects margin/price-sensitivity, not lower value. Validate with a live price test before locking.*

## Phase 4 — Meta ads targeting viability

Verified against `strategy/research/meta-job-titles.md` (**Category 3 — Beauty & aesthetics**, flagged in the master file as the **best-fit category for Meta**).

- **Job-title / interest availability:** **the strongest of any category.** "**Hairdresser / Hair stylist / Hair salon**" is a **🟢 reliable topic interest** — large and highly engaged in AU. Critically, the master file notes beauty interests are **unusually owner-adjacent**: beauty professionals actively follow beauty topics, so the interest is *less* consumer-diluted than trades or hospitality interests. It is still a **narrowing/lookalike-seed signal**, but a better one than in any other vertical.
- **Behavioural targeting (the spine):** 🟢 reliable platform behaviours —
  - **Small business owners** (primary)
  - **New active business admins (<12 / <24 months)** — "just opened a salon, needs a site"
  - **Facebook Page admins** / Instagram business accounts — near-universal for this niche (salons run active social), a very strong ICP proxy
- **Recommended stack:** *Small business owners* (or *New active business admins*) **+ "Hairdresser/Hair salon" interest as a narrowing layer + tight suburb/postcode geo**, graduating to **AU 1–3% lookalikes** from converters once seed volume exists. Because this audience is Instagram-native, **Instagram placements and visual creative carry the campaign** more than for any trade.
- **Geographic targeting:** dense **suburb/postcode clusters** across metro + regional — demand is everywhere there are people; suburb-level granularity is both possible and ideal (salons are hyper-local).
- **Estimated CPM:** AU all-industry baseline ~**$9.80 (2026)**; **Beauty & Health runs hotter at ~$12.46 CPM** (more advertisers competing for the visual audience). Use **~$11–$12.50** as the working anchor for this niche. *(2026 AU Meta benchmark; re-verify in live Ads Manager.)*
- **Estimated CTR / CPL:** beauty creative is visually strong (before/after, transformations) → **healthy CTR**, but the **beauty/health CPA runs higher (~$25–$34)** due to competitive CPMs. For our *free-preview signup* (a lighter conversion than a purchase) expect **mid-range signup CPL**, helped by best-in-class creative but pressured by category CPM. Net: **CAC slightly higher on the cost side than electrician's efficient end, but offset by best-in-class targeting fit and conversion-friendly visual creative.**
- **Creative angle:** *"Your salon's website should be as beautiful as your work — see yours free in 3 hours, with online booking built in."* Lead with transformation imagery and the **online-booking** hook; Instagram-first placements.

**Verdict:** `meta_targeting_viable: true` — **the best-fit category for Meta.** A 🟢, relatively un-diluted interest *plus* the behavioural spine *plus* an Instagram-native, visually-motivated owner *plus* conversion-friendly creative. The only cost headwind is the elevated beauty-category CPM. **No Special Ad Category triggers** for the B2B website offer (note: cosmetic-*injectable*/skin-clinic creative faces policy scrutiny — not relevant to hair salons).

## Phase 5 — Category mapping & tuning

**Category mapping:** **`beauty-aesthetics`.** Hairdressing is the archetypal beauty-aesthetics business — a visual, appointment-based, brand-as-aesthetic service where the *portfolio of work* and *ease of booking* are the product. It anchors the category alongside barber, nails, lashes/brows, beautician and day spa. No new category required; **no `category_required: new` flag.**

**Niche-specific tuning within the `beauty-aesthetics` category:**

- **Colour emphasis:** within the beauty-aesthetics palette, lean **editorial and refined** — soft, sophisticated neutrals (warm stone, cream, charcoal) with a **single elegant accent** (muted rose/blush, sage, or a deep luxe tone like aubergine/forest) chosen to feel salon-premium, not clinical. Generous whitespace, large imagery, and elegant serif/clean-sans pairing. The site must read as **aspirational and tactile**, not utilitarian — the opposite of the trades' high-visibility/safety palette.
- **Critical sections (prominence order):**
  1. **Online booking — above the fold, persistent "Book Now" CTA** (real-time calendar embed or deep-link to Fresha/Timely/Square). This is the highest-converting element and the single most important section for the niche.
  2. **Visual gallery / portfolio of work** — full-bleed, high-quality before/after and finished-look imagery; the credibility engine for a visual service.
  3. **Services + price list** — clear, scannable menu (cut, colour, balayage, foils, treatments, styling, extensions; men's/barber where relevant) with indicative pricing.
  4. **Instagram feed integration** — live feed pulling their curated portfolio; salons live on Instagram, so the site should mirror and amplify it.
  5. **Service-area / local pages** (programmatic local SEO: "balayage [suburb]", "[suburb] hair salon") — hyper-local intent capture.
  6. **Reviews / Google rating** block + **team/stylist bios** (clients follow individual stylists).
- **Trust signal priorities:** **Google rating + volume of reviews** (top priority for a personal-service purchase), **portfolio quality** (itself the strongest trust signal), **stylist credentials/specialisms** (e.g. colour specialist, extensions-certified, brand affiliations like Olaplex/Wella/Kérastase stockist), years established, and **awards/feature mentions** where present. No statutory licence to display (unlike electricians) — trust is **reputational and visual**, not regulatory.
- **Content tone:** **warm, confident, aspirational, personal.** First-person salon voice ("we") that conveys expertise and welcome; less formal than allied-health, warmer and more lifestyle-led than trades. Sell the *experience and the transformation*, not just the service.
- **Image style cues (stock fallback):** bright, modern salon interiors; close-ups of finished hair (colour, balayage, styling); a stylist at work; clean product flat-lays; natural light, editorial framing. Avoid dated/clip-art salon imagery and harsh clinical lighting. Prefer real-portfolio uploads over stock wherever the owner supplies them — authentic work outperforms stock decisively in this niche.
- **Schema.org subtype:** **`HairSalon`** (subtype of `LocalBusiness` / `HealthAndBeautyBusiness`), with `Service` markup on service pages, `priceRange`, `openingHours`, `aggregateRating`, and `areaServed` on local pages; `potentialAction` → `ReserveAction` for the booking link.

**Build dependency:** a `beauty-aesthetics` directory exists at `/templates/beauty-aesthetics/index.tsx`, but it is currently a **single-line stub** (one comment, no implemented component) — there is **no real beauty-aesthetics template and no multi-page site system** (no `/templates/categories/`, no `SiteProps`). The only substantively built template is the **single-page trades** component (`/templates/tradies/page.tsx`, ~798 lines). **Flag: the `beauty-aesthetics` category must be built from scratch as a multi-page site system (home + services + service-detail + gallery + booking + location/service-area + about) before this niche can be generated as specified.** This is a **category-level build dependency** (more work than electrician's, which only needed the existing trades component expanded), not a hairdresser-specific one.

## Phase 6 — Niche-specific upsells

- **SEO module:** **medium-high.** "Hairdresser near me / [suburb] hair salon / balayage [suburb] / men's haircut [suburb]" carries **strong, continuous local search volume** — programmatic service-area pages have real upside. Slightly behind trades only because a meaningful share of salon discovery happens on **Instagram and word-of-mouth** rather than search. Still a headline upsell.
- **Local Services Ads (LSA):** **NOT eligible / not applicable.** Hairdressing is **not** a Google LSA / Google Guaranteed vertical (LSA is restricted largely to home-services, legal, health and similar categories). This is a real **upsell-ceiling difference vs electrician** (which had confirmed LSA eligibility) and is the main reason the Scale tier is priced lower. Do **not** position any "Google Guaranteed" hook for this niche. *(Verify against Google's current LSA eligibility list at build time.)*
- **Review acquisition:** **high — arguably the single highest-value upsell.** Salon purchase decisions are reputation-driven; Google rating and review volume directly lift both organic conversion and map-pack ranking. Automated review requests post-appointment fit the recurring-visit cadence perfectly.
- **Niche-specific upsells (the core differentiators):**
  - **Online booking integration** — the flagship upsell for this niche (Fresha/Timely/Square embed or native calendar). Booking *is* the conversion; owning it (vs Fresha's per-client commission) is a real wedge.
  - **Instagram feed sync / portfolio auto-import** — keep the site visually fresh from their existing Instagram with zero effort.
  - **Rebooking / appointment-reminder automation** (SMS/email) — directly lifts the retention metric that drives salon LTV.
  - **Managed Meta/Instagram ads** — natural for an Instagram-native owner; the highest-fit paid upsell (replaces LSA as the Scale-tier headline).
  - **Loyalty / package promotion** support — supports the retention engine.

## Phase 7 — Unit economics

*All inputs flagged as estimates; validate against live cohort data.*

**CAC** = cost per free-preview signup ÷ free→paid conversion. Best-in-class targeting fit and strong visual creative push CPL down; the **elevated beauty-category CPM (~$12.46)** pushes it back up — net **mid-range** AU signup CPL.

| Scenario | Signup CPL | Free→paid conv. | **CAC** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bear | $38 | 6% | **~$633** |
| Base | $25 | 10% | **~$250** |
| Bull | $14 | 15% | **~$93** |

**LTV** = entry $39/mo × projected tenure (blended up for upgrades to growth/scale and the high underlying client-retention culture, but tempered by mid-margin price-sensitivity and a lower upsell ceiling than trades — no LSA).

| Scenario | Tenure | Notes | **LTV** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bear | 9 mo | high churn, price-sensitive micro-salon / chair-renter | **~$351** |
| Base | 18 mo | entry→partial upgrade, blended ARPU ~$48 | **~$864** |
| Bull | 30+ mo | upgrades to growth/scale (booking + ads), strong retention culture | **~$2,000+** |

**LTV:CAC** — Bear **~0.55:1 (fails)** · Base **~3.5:1 (clears the 3:1 target)** · Bull **~21:1.**

**Payback period:** base CAC $250 ÷ ~$48 blended ARPU ≈ **5.2 months** (vs electrician's 4.0; higher CPM + lower ARPU than trades).

**Read:** base-case economics **clear the 3:1 bar (~3.5:1)** but with **less headroom than electrician** — driven by (a) best-in-class Meta targeting + conversion-friendly visual creative, offset by (b) elevated beauty-category CPM, (c) a lower entry price and ARPU (mid-margin, price-sensitive), and (d) a lower upsell ceiling (no LSA). The strong underlying **client-retention culture** of salons is the lever that can push tenure (and LTV) toward the bull case — demonstrated bookings/calls and rebooking automation are the retention levers to watch. The bear case fails on churn among price-sensitive micro-salons, so onboarding, visible booking volume, and the booking-ownership wedge against Fresha are the activation priorities.

## Phase 8 — Competitive landscape

- **Niche-specific tooling:** the salon software incumbents — **Fresha, Timely, Square Appointments, Kitomba, Phorest** — own the **booking layer** but are not website builders; several (Fresha, Timely) bundle a **basic booking microsite**, which is the closest direct competitor to our owned-site value. Fresha's marketplace + **~20% new-client commission** is the key vulnerability to position against ("own your bookings, no commission").
- **Generic builders with salon presence:** **Wix and Squarespace** ($30–$50/mo) market salon templates aggressively and are the default DIY choice; **Durable** (AI builder) entering AU SMB. These are the real "free/cheap toy" alternatives to undercut on quality and speed.
- **Local Australian agencies:** numerous AU agencies build salon sites at **$500–$1,000 (template) to $5,000–$15,000 (custom)**, often with booking integration and ongoing maintenance retainers — they compete on bespoke design but not on speed or price.
- **Specialist salon marketers:** a cottage industry of salon-marketing consultants (loyalty, Instagram, ads) validates that the **review/ads/retention upsells** have a paying market.
- **Pricing benchmark:** managed ~$99/mo to beat; DIY $30–50/mo floor; booking software $42–79/staff/mo (the spend we sit beside); agency $500–15k one-off.
- **Differentiating angle for Preview Factory:** **an AI-generated, salon-true preview live in ~3 hours — visually matching the quality of their Instagram — with online booking built in, programmatic local-SEO service-area pages, Instagram-feed sync and review acquisition**, framed as *"a website as beautiful as your work, that takes bookings"* — at **$39 entry**, well under both the $99 managed pack and agency builds, with an Instagram-ads upsell path. Differentiation = *visual quality (the thing this owner cares about most)* + *booking ownership (vs Fresha commission)* + *speed-to-see-it* + *price*.

## Phase 9 — Decision

**Decision: GO.**

Hairdressing is a strong v1 beauty niche and the natural anchor for the `beauty-aesthetics` category. It clears the screen comfortably and clears the **3:1 LTV:CAC bar in the base case (~3.5:1, ~5.2-month payback)**. Its standout strength is **Meta fit**: this is explicitly the **best-fit category for Meta** in the master file — a usable, relatively un-diluted 🟢 interest plus the behavioural spine plus an **Instagram-native, visually-motivated owner** whose biggest weakness is precisely an owned website, which is exactly what we sell. The pitch ("a website as beautiful as your work, with booking built in") lands directly on what this owner values, and the **booking-ownership wedge against Fresha's commission** is a genuine differentiator. Against electrician, the economics have **less headroom** — mid-margin price-sensitivity forces a lower $39 entry and ARPU, the beauty-category CPM (~$12.46) runs hot, and there is **no LSA upsell** (lowering the Scale ceiling) — so the base case is solid but not flagship-strong, and the bear case fails on micro-salon churn. The principal blocker is the **largest build dependency seen so far**: the `beauty-aesthetics` category is currently only a single-line stub and must be **built from scratch as a multi-page site system** (with first-class online-booking and gallery components) before this niche can be generated as specified.

## Phase 10 — Implementation priority

- **Priority tier:** **Tier 1** (launch in first 90 days) — best-in-class Meta fit, large stable market, strong product-need (great Instagram / poor website), and the category anchor that unlocks every other beauty niche (barber, nails, lashes, beautician). Sequence it **after** the trades build only because its build dependency is heavier.
- **Build requirements:** **category-level and substantial.** Build the `beauty-aesthetics` category as a **multi-page site system** from the current stub — home + services + service-detail + **gallery/portfolio** + **online-booking** + location/service-area + about — under the new `SiteProps` schema. Two **first-class components are mandatory and niche-defining**: (1) an **online-booking embed/CTA** system (Fresha/Timely/Square deep-link or native calendar) and (2) a **visual gallery + Instagram-feed integration**. Hairdresser-specific tuning (per Phase 5) is then applied at generation time via the niche system prompt — editorial palette, booking-first layout, services-with-prices menu, `HairSalon` schema.
- **Compliance considerations:** standard Meta ad policy; **no Special Ad Category triggers** (B2B website offer). No statutory licence claims to manage (unlike electricians) — trust signals are reputational/visual. Ensure any **brand-stockist or certification claims** on generated sites are **owner-supplied, not fabricated**. If the category later extends to **cosmetic injectables / skin clinics**, flag the separate Meta ad-policy scrutiny on that creative — **not applicable to hair salons.**
