# Niche Analysis — Physiotherapy / Physiotherapy Clinics

- **Niche slug:** `physiotherapy`
- **Category:** Allied health → maps to visual category **`allied-health`**
- **Analyst:** Preview Factory strategy
- **Date:** 2026-06-19
- **Screen result:** PASS (local service, GBP presence, owner-operated clinics, customer LTV well above $300, reachable via Meta interest + behavioural + geo targeting) — but with a **digital-sophistication caveat** (many clinics already have a real site) and an **Ahpra advertising-compliance overlay** that constrains site copy and review handling.
- **Methodology note:** First **allied-health** niche run under the revised methodology (Phase 5 = category mapping & tuning). Deliberately chosen as the calibration analysis for the allied-health category — a regulated, higher-willingness-to-pay vertical to contrast the trades calibration (`electrician`) and stress-test the "they already have a site" objection plus health-sensitive Meta and Ahpra constraints.

> **Currency:** all figures AUD. **Uncertainty is flagged inline** — Meta audience sizes, conversion and tenure assumptions, and per-clinic revenue/LTV are unvalidated and must be confirmed against live data before committing budget.

## Phase 1 — Market sizing

- **Estimated business count:** ~**9,497** physiotherapy-services businesses in Australia (IBISWorld, *Physiotherapy Services in Australia*, 2025), **up ~5.2% YoY** and growing ~**6.2% per year on average over 2020–2025**. A genuinely *expanding* business count — the opposite of the trades construction cycle — driven by ageing-population demand, NDIS/aged-care allied-health funding, and sports/musculoskeletal load.
- **Industry revenue:** ~**$3.9bn (2025)**, up ~2.9% YoY, a ~**4.2% CAGR (2020–2025)** (IBISWorld). Smaller total industry than electrical ($36bn) but far more clinics relative to revenue → a **lower average revenue per business** (see Phase 2).
- **Geographic distribution:** no clean per-niche ABS split available; physiotherapy demand tracks population, ageing and sporting participation, concentrating in **Greater Sydney (NSW), Melbourne (VIC), South-East Queensland (Brisbane/Gold Coast/Sunshine Coast)**, then **Perth (WA)** and **Adelaide (SA)**, with a meaningful regional tail (sports clubs, aged care, NDIS in regional centres). Demand is **established-suburb, not new-build-corridor** — every suburb with residents generates musculoskeletal, post-surgical, sports and aged-care demand. *(Triangulation only — confirm with ABS by SA4 before targeting spend.)*
- **Growth rate estimate:** **structurally positive.** Unlike construction-linked trades, physiotherapy rides demographic tailwinds (ageing population, chronic-disease management, NDIS/aged-care allied-health, growing sports-injury and preventative/Pilates-led demand). Ahpra registrant growth and IBISWorld's positive business-count and revenue trajectory both point the same way. A **durable-demand niche.**
- **Addressable subset (Meta-reachable):** Ahpra registers a large pool of physiotherapists nationally (tens of thousands of registrants per the Physiotherapy Board registration data table, 30 Sep 2025), but most are **employees, not clinic owners**. Against ~9,497 businesses, the realistic reachable **owner/principal pool with a findable digital footprint** (FB Page / GBP / recent business admin) is a **working estimate of 6,000–9,000 nationally** — a high *capture rate* of the business base (clinics are digitally present), though a smaller absolute pool than electricians' 15,000–25,000. *(Estimate — unverified; verify Ahpra registrant-to-owner ratio and live Ads Manager sizes.)*

## Phase 2 — Customer profile

- **Annual business revenue range:** industry average ≈ **$3.9bn ÷ 9,497 ≈ ~$410,000 revenue per business** (IBISWorld triangulation). The distribution is bimodal: **solo/sole-principal clinics ~$150k–$350k**, **multi-practitioner clinics (3–8 physios + admin) $500k–$1.5m+**. Owner principals commonly take home **$120k–$300k+** (financial-modelling triangulation — flagged as estimate). Higher revenue base than most trades → real headroom for a managed subscription, *but* see digital-sophistication caveat.
- **Team size:** **sole principal or small multi-practitioner practice** dominant, with an established tail of multi-site groups. The owner is a **practising physiotherapist** who also runs the business — time-poor, clinically focused, often with a **practice manager / front-desk admin** handling bookings and "marketing."
- **Decision-maker:** the **owner-principal physiotherapist**, frequently with a **practice manager or spouse/partner** influencing the website/marketing decision. Larger groups may have a dedicated practice manager as the operational buyer.
- **Current digital sophistication:** **MODERATE-TO-HIGH — the key differentiator from trades.** Most clinics already run **online booking (Cliniko / Halaxy / HealthEngine), HICAPS, an active GBP and an existing website** (often a templated or agency build). They are *not* "no site / Facebook-only." This **inverts the trades pitch**: the angle is **not** "you have no website" but **"your site doesn't convert / doesn't rank locally / isn't Ahpra-clean / looks dated next to the clinic down the road."** Quality and local-SEO depth, not mere existence, is the wedge.
- **Average customer transaction value:** **initial consult $95–$150; follow-ups $85–$120** (multiple AU clinic price guides, 2025). Medicare EPC/CDM rebate ~$56 for up to 5 sessions/year; private-health extras rebate a portion via HICAPS. So **per-visit ticket is modest** but **multi-visit by design**.
- **Customer lifetime value to the business:**
  - **Per episode of care:** a typical musculoskeletal episode runs **~4–8 visits** → **~$400–$900 per episode** (initial + follow-ups). *(Episode-length is a clinical-practice estimate, not a published figure — flagged.)*
  - **Across the relationship:** patients **return for new episodes over years** (recurring injuries, chronic management, family members, sports seasons, post-surgical rehab, Pilates/clinical-exercise memberships) → patient LTV commonly **$800–$3,000+**, with chronic/NDIS/aged-care and sports clients higher and more recurring.
  - Comfortably above the $300 screen threshold. **Crucially, demand is direct-to-end-patient search** ("physio near me / [suburb] physio / sports physio"), so a lead-gen, locally-ranked website captures a real, attributable slice of new patients — the core retention frame still applies.
- **Typical marketing budget per month:** **$300–$2,000+**, more planned and more sophisticated than trades — many clinics already buy SEO, Google Ads or a managed allied-health website (see Phase 8). A double-edged fact: **warmer to the value proposition, but a larger share is already serviced** by an incumbent.
- **Booking/inquiry cycle:** **scheduled and consultation-led** (book an initial assessment, then a planned course of follow-ups). **Not emergency-driven** like electrical fault calls — so the value frame is **"more new-patient bookings from your suburb"** and **"fill the diary,"** not "answer the 2am call." Online booking is the dominant conversion path, which raises the importance of a fast, booking-integrated, mobile-first site.

## Phase 3 — Willingness to pay

**Reference points (AUD):**

- **Allied-health-specific managed website vendors:** **~$495/month** care-plan tiers for solo clinics, often with a **~$995 ex-GST setup**; multi-location groups **$3,000–$5,000/month** (AU allied-health website specialists, 2026). This is a **materially higher managed benchmark than the ~$99/mo tradie packs** — allied health pays more for compliant, booking-integrated, locally-ranked sites.
- **Generic managed-SMB benchmark:** ~**$99/month** (the cross-niche managed anchor).
- **DIY builders (Wix / Squarespace):** **$30–$50/month** + many hours of clinician time (and Ahpra-compliance risk if self-authored).
- **Agency one-off build:** **$1,500–$3,000** template-based; **$5,000+** custom allied-health builds, frequently bundled with ongoing SEO retainers.
- **Existing spend:** clinics already commonly spend **$300–$2,000+/mo** on marketing/SEO/ads and **$495+/mo** with allied-health site vendors — so a site subscription is **small relative to current outlay**, and there is room to price **above** the trades entry without breaking the no-brainer test.
- **Value of one patient:** a single additional new patient (one episode ≈ $400–$900; relationship $800–$3,000+) returns **many months-to-years** of any tier below. ROI is not the constraint; the constraints are **incumbency** (they may already have a site) and **signalling** (price too low and a clinical owner reads it as a toy).

**Recommended tiers (AUD/month):**

| Tier | Price | Justification |
|---|---|---|
| **Entry** | **$69** | Set **above** the trades $49 entry and well above the $30–50 DIY floor because physios have a **higher revenue base (~$410k avg), higher willingness-to-pay, and existing $300–$2,000/mo marketing budgets** — and because in a regulated, professional vertical, **too-low signals "toy."** Still a dramatic undercut of the ~$495/mo allied-health managed benchmark, so it remains a clear no-brainer (one extra patient/year returns many×). Pitched as "managed, Ahpra-aware, booking-ready — at a fraction of an agency care plan." |
| **Growth** | **$129** | Adds programmatic **local service-area + condition pages** (e.g. sports injury, lower-back, post-surgical rehab, women's health), **review acquisition (Ahpra-compliant — service-experience only, not outcome claims)**, and online-booking integration. Anchors against the lower edge of the allied-health managed market (~$495) while costing a quarter of it; targets clinics actively chasing new-patient volume. |
| **Scale** | **$249** | Adds Google/Meta **ads management + SEO management + multi-location/practitioner pages + booking-funnel optimisation**. Justified for multi-physio and multi-site groups where a single managed new patient is worth hundreds-to-thousands in episode + relationship value, and who today pay $3,000–$5,000/mo to allied-health agencies. Self-serve-priced disruption of the agency retainer. |

*Flagged uncertainty: tier prices are reasoned against market anchors (notably the ~$495/mo allied-health managed benchmark), not tested. Validate with a live price test before locking. Note the **higher entry than trades is deliberate** — driven by willingness-to-pay and signalling, and offset by higher incumbency risk.*

## Phase 4 — Meta ads targeting viability

Verified against `strategy/research/meta-job-titles.md` (Category 2 — Health & allied health).

- **Job-title / interest availability:** **usable.** "Physiotherapy / Physiotherapist" is a **🟢 reliable topic interest** in AU per the master file — strong, well-populated — but, as noted there, it **mixes patients and practitioners** (consumer-contaminated, even more so than trades because patients vastly outnumber owners). The interest is a **narrowing / lookalike-seed signal, not the spine.**
- **Health-sensitive targeting constraint (important):** Meta **removed health-condition-based sensitive targeting**, so you **cannot** target by injury/condition (e.g. "back pain," "sports injury"). Only the **profession topic interest** remains, plus the behavioural layer. Plan creative and audience accordingly — the *creative* carries condition messaging, the *targeting* cannot.
- **Behavioural targeting (the spine):** 🟢 reliable platform behaviours —
  - **Small business owners** (primary)
  - **Business decision-makers** (relevant for multi-practitioner/practice-manager buyers)
  - **New active business admins (<12 / <24 months)** — newer clinics needing a first proper site
  - **Facebook Page admins** — proxy for "runs a clinic with a FB presence"
- **Recommended stack:** *Small business owners* (or *Business decision-makers*) **+ "Physiotherapy/Physiotherapist" interest as a narrowing layer + tight suburb/postcode geo**, graduating to **AU 1–3% lookalikes from clinic-owner leads/converters** as soon as seed volume exists — **strongly preferred here**, per the master file's allied-health verdict, because the topic interest is so patient-heavy.
- **Geographic targeting:** metro + regional **suburb/postcode clusters** around clinic catchments; suburb-level where audience size allows.
- **Estimated CPM:** AU all-industry baseline ~**$9.80 (2026)**; **healthcare runs above average** (specialised targeting + compliance review). Use a working **CPM ~$11–$18** for health creative. *(2026 AU Meta benchmark; re-verify in live Ads Manager.)*
- **Estimated CTR / CPL:** healthcare on Meta in AU is among the **costlier** sectors — cosmetic/medical CPLs cited at **$40–$80**, healthcare CPA ~**$157**. Our offer is **B2B (a website for the clinic owner)**, not a patient-acquisition health ad, which should **avoid the worst health-CPL penalty** — but expect CPLs **above the trades range** given (a) a smaller owner pool, (b) consumer-contaminated interest, and (c) health-creative review friction.
- **Creative angle:** *"Get more new physio patients booking in [suburb] — see your new website free in 3 hours."* Lead on **booking/new-patient volume and local ranking**, not "you have no site" (most do). Keep creative **Ahpra-aware**: no outcome/cure claims, no patient testimonials in the ad.

**Verdict:** `meta_targeting_viable: true` (interest + behaviour). Targeting is workable — a usable 🟢 interest plus the behavioural spine plus lookalikes — but **expected CAC is higher than electrician** due to a smaller owner pool, heavier consumer contamination of the interest, health-sensitive targeting limits (no condition targeting), and higher health CPMs/CPLs.

## Phase 5 — Category mapping & tuning

**Category mapping:** **`allied-health`.** Physiotherapy is a **registered, regulated allied-health profession** (Ahpra-registered) delivering clinical, appointment-based, rebate-eligible care from a clinic — the defining profile of the `allied-health` visual category (alongside chiropractic, podiatry, dietetics, psychology, OT, dental-adjacent). It is **not** a trade (no licensed building work, no emergency call-out, no "more local jobs" framing) and **not** beauty-aesthetics (clinical/medical, not cosmetic). **No new category required; no `category_required: new` flag.** Allied-health is the correct home.

**Niche-specific tuning within the `allied-health` category:**

- **Colour emphasis:** within the allied-health palette, lean **calm, clean, clinical-but-warm** — a trustworthy **medical teal / soft blue or sage-green primary** with a warm neutral base and a single restrained accent. Convey **competence + reassurance + movement/recovery** (avoid the trades' high-vis amber and the beauty category's saturated/luxe tones). Clinical credibility over visual flash.
- **Critical sections (prominence order):**
  1. **Online booking CTA** (book an appointment) — **above the fold, click-to-book** is the single highest-converting element for this scheduled, booking-led niche; integrate with Cliniko/Halaxy/HealthEngine where present.
  2. **Conditions / what we treat** (sports injuries, lower-back/neck pain, post-surgical rehab, women's/pelvic health, NDIS/aged-care, clinical Pilates/exercise) — patients self-identify by problem; structure as the services grid.
  3. **Practitioner profiles + qualifications + Ahpra registration** — clinical trust is *person-led*; show physios, credentials and **Ahpra registration**.
  4. **Health-fund / rebate / HICAPS + Medicare info** (on-the-spot claiming, EPC/CDM, private-health rebates) — a primary practical conversion driver for patients.
  5. **Service-area / location pages** (programmatic local SEO across catchment suburbs).
  6. **Reviews block — Ahpra-compliant** (service-experience reviews only: friendliness, wait times, ease of booking; **no clinical-outcome testimonials**; ability to disable/curate reviews to stay compliant).
- **Trust signal priorities:** **Ahpra registration** (top — display registration status), **APA (Australian Physiotherapy Association) membership**, **HICAPS / health-fund-rebate / Medicare-EPC acceptance**, **NDIS-/aged-care-registered** where applicable, years of experience, professional indemnity/insurance, university qualifications. (Note: trust signals here are **registration- and association-based**, not licence-number-based like trades.)
- **Content tone:** **professional, reassuring, clinically credible, warm** — "qualified, caring, evidence-based, here to get you moving again." More **formal and outcome-careful** than trades' direct safety voice and warmer than professional-services' corporate tone. **Compliance-aware copy is mandatory** (see below).
- **Image style cues (stock fallback):** clean modern clinic/treatment rooms, a physio guiding a patient through an exercise, hands-on assessment, rehab/exercise equipment and Pilates reformers, bright and calm. Diverse, real-looking practitioners and patients. **Avoid** graphic injury imagery, "miracle recovery" framing, or anything implying guaranteed outcomes.
- **Schema.org subtype:** **`Physiotherapy`** (subtype of `MedicalBusiness` / `LocalBusiness`), with `MedicalClinic`/`Physician`-style markup for practitioners, `Service` for treatments, and `areaServed` on service-area pages.

**Ahpra advertising-compliance overlay (category-defining for allied-health, applies to every generated physio site):**
- **No testimonials about clinical care/outcomes** — the National Law prohibits testimonials for regulated health services. Generated copy and any reviews module must **exclude outcome/quality-of-care statements**; service-experience reviews (friendliness, wait times, booking ease) are permitted. Ahpra has actively pursued physiotherapist advertising complaints (49 in ~18 months), so this is **enforced, not theoretical**.
- **No misleading/exaggerated claims, no "cure"/guaranteed-outcome language, no claims that create unreasonable expectation of benefit.** The generation prompt must hard-block these.
- **Owner-supplied, verifiable credentials only** — Ahpra registration, qualifications and association memberships must come from the owner, never fabricated.

**Build dependency:** the `allied-health` category currently exists only as a **single-line stub** (`/templates/allied-health/index.tsx`, ~1 line — a placeholder, **not a functional template**). There is **no multi-page allied-health site system**, **no `/templates/categories/` directory**, and **no `SiteProps` schema** anywhere in `/templates/`. The only substantive template is the single-page trades component (`/templates/tradies/page.tsx`, ~798 lines). **Flag: the entire `allied-health` multi-page site system must be built from scratch (home + booking + conditions/services + service-detail + practitioner profiles + location/service-area + rebates/health-funds + FAQ + about), with the Ahpra-compliance constraints baked into the schema and generation prompt, before this niche can be generated as specified.** This is a **category-level build dependency (build needed)** — larger than electrician's "expand existing single-page to multi-page," because allied-health has **no real template at all yet**.

## Phase 6 — Niche-specific upsells

- **SEO module:** **high.** "Physio near me / [suburb] physio / sports physiotherapist [suburb] / [condition] physio" carries **strong, continuous local search volume with direct booking intent** — programmatic service-area + condition pages have real upside. A headline upsell, comparable to electrician's SEO case.
- **Local Services Ads (LSA):** **LOW / likely ineligible.** Google's LSA "Health" categories in Australia are limited and **physiotherapy is not a confirmed LSA-eligible vertical** (LSA's healthcare presence centres on a narrow set of categories and varies by market). **Do not promise Google-Guaranteed/LSA leads for physio** — unlike electrician (confirmed LSA-eligible), this upsell is **not available** here. *(Verify against Google's current LSA eligibility list at signup; treat as ineligible unless confirmed.)*
- **Review acquisition:** **medium — and compliance-gated.** Reviews lift local ranking and conversion, **but Ahpra prohibits clinical-outcome testimonials.** The review tool must **steer patients toward service-experience feedback** (booking, staff, facilities) and **support disabling/curating** outcome statements. Positioned correctly this is a real upsell; positioned naively it is a compliance liability. **Differentiator: an Ahpra-aware review tool** competitors' generic tools don't offer.
- **Niche-specific upsells:**
  - **Online-booking integration** (Cliniko / Halaxy / HealthEngine) — the single most valuable add-on for a booking-led niche; deep-link or embed the clinic's booking system.
  - **Condition / treatment landing pages** (sports injury, back pain, post-surgical rehab, women's/pelvic health, clinical Pilates) — captures problem-led search.
  - **New-patient intake / pre-screening forms** (digital intake, consent, history) — reduces front-desk load.
  - **Telehealth booking page** + **NDIS/aged-care referral capture** pages for clinics serving funded clients.

## Phase 7 — Unit economics

*All inputs flagged as estimates; validate against live cohort data.*

**CAC** = cost per free-preview signup ÷ free→paid conversion. Smaller owner pool, consumer-contaminated interest, no condition targeting and higher health CPMs/CPLs push signup CPL **above the trades range**; offsetting this, clinics are digitally engaged (respond to a quality/local-ranking pitch) and willingness-to-pay is higher.

| Scenario | Signup CPL | Free→paid conv. | **CAC** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bear | $55 | 6% | **~$917** |
| Base | $35 | 10% | **~$350** |
| Bull | $20 | 15% | **~$133** |

**LTV** = entry $69/mo × projected tenure (blended up for upgrades to growth/scale, which a meaningful share of clinics reach given higher willingness-to-pay; blended ARPU assumed ~$85 at base given the higher tier ladder).

| Scenario | Tenure | Notes | **LTV** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bear | 9 mo | high churn; clinic reverts to incumbent site / agency | **~$621** |
| Base | 20 mo | entry→partial upgrade, blended ARPU ~$85 | **~$1,700** |
| Bull | 32+ mo | upgrades to growth/scale (SEO + ads + multi-site) | **~$3,500+** |

**LTV:CAC** — Bear **~0.7:1 (fails)** · Base **~4.9:1 (clears the 3:1 target)** · Bull **~26:1.**

**Payback period:** base CAC $350 ÷ ~$85 blended ARPU ≈ **4.1 months** (comparable to electrician's 4.0).

**Read:** base-case economics **clear the 3:1 bar (~4.9:1, ~4-month payback)**, driven by **higher willingness-to-pay and a higher tier ladder ($69/$129/$249)** that lift LTV enough to absorb a structurally **higher CAC** than trades. The model is **more CAC-sensitive than electrician** (smaller owner pool, pricier health media, no condition targeting), so the **bear case fails on the combination of high CPL and churn**. The decisive levers are: (1) **lookalike audiences from clinic-owner leads** to escape the patient-contaminated interest and pull CPL down, (2) **a quality/local-ranking creative angle** (not "you have no site") to convert a digitally-sophisticated, often-incumbent buyer, and (3) **demonstrated new-patient booking volume** to defend tenure against the existing-agency/site incumbent.

## Phase 8 — Competitive landscape

- **Allied-health-specific website vendors:** a **dedicated, mature AU field** — allied-health website specialists (e.g. Practice Lab / "Therapy Websites," Allied Health Website Designs, Clare Walter Design, Webstallion, and physio-focused agencies) offering **Ahpra-compliant, booking-integrated** sites at **~$495/mo care plans (+ ~$995 setup) for solo clinics and $3,000–$5,000/mo for groups.** This is a **more sophisticated and more entrenched competitive set than the tradie-site field** — they already solve compliance and booking integration, which is the bar to clear.
- **Specialist physio marketers:** several AU agencies run **SEO / Google Ads / digital marketing specifically for physiotherapists** (e.g. Dezign Digital and similar), validating a real **ads/SEO upsell market** — but also meaning the upsell tier competes against established retainers.
- **Generic builders:** **Wix / Squarespace** ($30–$50/mo DIY, compliance risk if self-authored); **Durable** and similar AI builders entering AU SMB (generic, not Ahpra-aware).
- **Practice-management incumbents:** **Cliniko / Halaxy / HealthEngine** own the booking layer; many clinics treat the booking tool as primary and the website as secondary — integration (not replacement) is the play.
- **Pricing benchmark:** **~$495/mo allied-health managed care plan** is the relevant benchmark to beat (not the ~$99 tradie pack); DIY $30–50/mo floor; group/agency retainers $3,000–$5,000/mo (justifies our Scale tier).
- **Differentiating angle for Preview Factory:** **AI-generated, physio-true, Ahpra-aware preview live in ~3 hours, free trial, with programmatic local-SEO service-area + condition pages, online-booking integration, practitioner/qualification + Ahpra-registration trust blocks, HICAPS/rebate messaging, and call/booking tracking** — framed as *"more new patients booking from your suburb"* — at **$69 entry, a fraction of the ~$495/mo allied-health care plan**, with an upgrade path into managed SEO/ads that undercuts the agency retainers at self-serve pricing. Differentiation = **speed-to-see-it + local-SEO/condition-page depth + built-in Ahpra compliance + price**, aimed squarely at the "I already have a site but it doesn't bring me patients" clinic.

## Phase 9 — Decision

**Decision: GO.**

Physiotherapy clears the screen and clears the **3:1 LTV:CAC bar in the base case (~4.9:1, ~4-month payback)**. It is a **structurally growing** niche (business count +5.2% YoY, ~6.2%/yr over five years; demographic + NDIS/aged-care + sports tailwinds), with **direct-to-end-patient search demand** so a locally-ranked, booking-integrated site captures attributable new patients, and **higher willingness-to-pay** than trades (avg ~$410k revenue, $300–$2,000+/mo existing marketing, a ~$495/mo allied-health managed benchmark to undercut) — supporting the higher **$69/$129/$249** tier ladder. The economics are nonetheless **more CAC-sensitive than electrician**: a **smaller owner pool**, **consumer-contaminated Meta interest**, **health-sensitive targeting limits (no condition targeting)** and **higher health CPMs/CPLs** push CAC up, and the **bear case fails** on high CPL + churn — making **clinic-owner lookalikes**, a **quality/local-ranking creative angle**, and **demonstrated booking volume** the decisive levers. Two niche-specific complications shape the GO: **(1) higher digital sophistication / incumbency** — most clinics already have a site, so the pitch is "convert/rank/compliant," not "you have none"; and **(2) Ahpra advertising compliance** — no clinical-outcome testimonials, no cure/guarantee claims, owner-supplied credentials only, which must be **baked into the schema, generation prompt and review tool**. The principal blocker is a **category-level build dependency**: the `allied-health` category is currently a one-line stub with **no multi-page site system, no `/templates/categories/`, and no `SiteProps`** — the allied-health template must be **built from scratch** (with compliance constraints) before this niche can be generated as specified.
