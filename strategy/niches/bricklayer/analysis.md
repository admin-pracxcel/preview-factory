# Niche Analysis — Bricklaying / Blockwork

- **Niche slug:** `bricklayer`
- **Category:** Trades (construction & building)
- **Analyst:** Preview Factory strategy
- **Date:** 2026-06-19
- **Screen result:** PASS (local service, GBP presence, owner-operated, customer LTV well above $300, reachable via Meta behavioural + geo targeting)

> **Currency:** all figures AUD. **Uncertainty is flagged inline** — Meta audience sizes and our own conversion/tenure assumptions are unvalidated and must be confirmed against live data before committing budget.

---

## Phase 1 — Market sizing

- **Estimated business count:** ~**9,297** bricklaying-services businesses in Australia (IBISWorld, *Bricklaying Services in Australia*). Business numbers have been essentially flat, ~0.3% CAGR 2019–2024.
- **Industry revenue:** ~**$3.5bn (2024)**, ~$3.9–4.0bn (2025–26 IBISWorld estimate), with revenue declining at ~0.7% CAGR over the prior five years — a **mature, flat-to-declining industry** tightly coupled to residential construction cycles.
- **Geographic distribution:** no clean per-niche ABS split was available; bricklaying tracks residential/commercial construction activity, which concentrates in the high-growth corridors of **Greater Sydney (NSW), Melbourne (VIC), and South-East Queensland (Brisbane/Gold Coast/Sunshine Coast)**, followed by **Perth (WA)** and **Adelaide (SA)**. Outer-metro growth suburbs (new estates) are the densest job sources. *(Triangulation only — flag as unverified; confirm with ABS Building Approvals by SA4 before targeting spend.)*
- **Growth rate estimate:** flat. ~0.3% business-count CAGR, slightly negative revenue CAGR. Not a tailwind niche.
- **Addressable subset (Meta-reachable):** the realistic reachable owner pool is well below the 9,297 business count. Many "bricklayers" are individual employees/labourers, not business owners; of the businesses, a subset run a Facebook Page / are recent business admins (our true behavioural targets). **Working estimate: 3,000–5,000 owner-operators with a findable digital footprint nationally** — modest, and it depends almost entirely on behavioural targeting since no usable "bricklayer" interest exists (see Phase 4). *(Estimate — unverified.)*

## Phase 2 — Customer profile

- **Annual business revenue range:** sole-trader bricklayers commonly **$100k–$250k**; small crews (2–5) up to ~$0.5m–$1m. *(Trade-press/accounting-guide range; unverified at ABS level.)*
- **Team size:** overwhelmingly **sole trader or small crew**. The owner is on the tools.
- **Decision-maker:** the **owner** (the bricklayer), frequently with a **spouse/partner handling admin, quotes and any "marketing."** Marketing decisions are ad hoc and made by one or both.
- **Current digital sophistication:** **low.** Many have **no website or a Facebook-only presence**; a GBP listing often exists but is unmanaged. Work has historically come through **builder relationships and word of mouth**, not search — this is the central strategic fact for this niche.
- **Average customer transaction value:** wide. Residential jobs (feature walls, extensions, fences, retaining, full-house brickwork) typically **$2,000–$20,000**; small repointing/repair jobs **$500–$3,000**.
- **Customer lifetime value to the business:** two distinct customer types —
  - **One-off homeowners:** low repeat, high ticket → LTV ≈ one job ($2k–$10k+).
  - **Builders / subcontract relationships:** recurring, the backbone of most brickies' books → LTV easily **$10k+/year**.
  Either way, customer LTV is **far above the $300 screen threshold.** *(But note: because so much demand is builder-referral, a consumer-facing lead-gen website captures a smaller share of total demand than in trades that sell direct to homeowners — material for Phase 7/9.)*
- **Typical marketing budget per month:** minimal and irregular — **$0–$500**, often $0. Spend is reactive (a slow month) rather than planned.
- **Booking/inquiry cycle:** **quote/consultation-led and project-scheduled.** Not an emergency/instant-call trade (unlike plumbing/electrical). Customers request a quote, compare, and schedule weeks out.

## Phase 3 — Willingness to pay

**Reference points (AUD):**

- **Managed tradie website services:** ~**$99/month** (e.g. heytradie, busi.au, Tradiecard and similar AU tradie-site providers), typically no/low setup.
- **DIY builders (Wix / Squarespace):** **$30–$50/month** + 15–80 hours of the owner's own time over the site's life.
- **Agency one-off build:** **$1,500–$3,000** template-based; **$5,000+** custom.
- **Value of one job:** a single additional residential job ($2,000+) returns **multiple years** of any subscription tier below — ROI is not the constraint; *perceived* value and price-sensitivity are.

**Constraints specific to this niche:** low marketing sophistication, thin/cyclical margins in a flat industry, and skepticism that "a website" produces jobs (because their jobs come from builders). The entry price must be an obvious no-brainer and must visibly beat the $99 managed benchmark.

**Recommended tiers (AUD/month):**

| Tier | Price | Justification |
|---|---|---|
| **Entry** | **$39** | Undercuts the ~$99 managed-competitor benchmark by ~60% and sits just above pure-DIY ($30–50) while removing all the DIY labour — a genuine no-brainer for a low-sophistication owner. One job/year ($2k+) returns ~4× the annual cost. Higher than the $19–29 floor used for low-margin hospitality because the delivered ticket value here is far larger and $39 still signals "managed, not toy." |
| **Growth** | **$79** | Adds local-SEO service-area pages + review acquisition. Still below a single managed competitor's $99 while delivering more (programmatic local pages + reviews). Targets owners actively chasing direct residential work. |
| **Scale** | **$149** | Adds Meta/Google ads management + quote tools. Only justified for crews pursuing direct-to-homeowner volume; most sole-trader brickies will not reach this tier. |

*Flagged uncertainty: tier prices are reasoned against market anchors, not tested. Validate with a live price test before locking.*

## Phase 4 — Meta ads targeting viability

Verified against `strategy/research/meta-job-titles.md` (Category 1 — Trades).

- **Job-title / interest availability:** **none usable.** "Bricklayer / Concreter / Landscaper (hardscape)" is flagged **🔴 Unreliable** — effectively no populated AU interest exists. There is **no interest-targeting spine** for this niche.
- **Behavioural targeting (the actual spine):** 🟢 reliable platform behaviours apply to any trade —
  - **Small business owners** (primary)
  - **New active business admins (<12 / <24 months)** — strong "just started, needs a site" signal
  - **Facebook Page admins** — proxy for "runs a business with a FB presence, weak/no website" = our ICP
- **Geographic targeting:** AU outer-metro **growth-corridor suburb/postcode clusters** (new-estate areas in Greater Sydney, Melbourne, SE-QLD, Perth) where new residential brickwork concentrates. Suburb-level where audience size allows; otherwise SA3/region clusters.
- **Lookalike seed strategy:** **the recommended long-run spine.** Seed AU 1–3% lookalikes from preview viewers, lead-form openers, and (once available) paying-customer lists. Until seed volume exists, run behaviour + geo.
- **Estimated CPM:** AU baseline ~**$11** (2026 range ~$10.68–$14.34). Australia runs ~21% below the global CPM benchmark — a cost advantage.
- **Estimated CTR / CPL:** trades/home-services local lead creative can reach **$8–$12 CPL** at the efficient end, with a broader AU range of **$20–$80** (mid ~$40). For *our* B2B owner-targeting (behaviour-only, no interest precision) expect the **higher/mid end**.
- **Creative does the qualifying** that targeting can't: angle on *"Get more brick & blockwork jobs in [suburb] — see your new website free in 3 hours."*

**Verdict:** `meta_targeting_viable: true — behaviour-only.` Reachable via Small business owners / New active business admins / Page admins + tight geo + lookalikes, but with **no interest-level precision**, so CAC will run higher than interest-targetable niches and creative carries the load. Per the master file's rule, this is *not* a false flag (a strong behavioural proxy exists), but it is a weaker targeting profile than most trades.

## Phase 5 — Template requirements

- **Design language:** **solid, trustworthy, craftsmanship-forward.** Heavy, grounded feel — masonry is about durability and skill.
- **Colour palette:** earthen/masonry tones — terracotta/clay red, charcoal, warm grey, sandstone, with a strong neutral base. Avoid clinical or playful palettes.
- **Photography style:** real **brick/block textures**, completed walls, and **before/after** of restoration/repointing. Hero on a clean finished feature wall. Authenticity over stock.
- **Critical sections:**
  - **Portfolio / gallery with before-after** (the primary trust driver for masonry).
  - **Service-area pages** (programmatic local SEO across growth-corridor suburbs).
  - **Services** (face brickwork, blockwork, retaining/fencing, repointing/restoration, feature walls).
  - **Quote-request CTA** + call tracking (consultation-led cycle).
  - **Builder / trade references** module (reflects how the niche actually wins work).
- **Content tone:** **direct, plain-spoken, authoritative** — no corporate gloss.
- **Trust signals:** trade licence/registration where applicable, **public liability insurance**, white card, years of experience, builder references, association/membership badges.
- **Schema.org type:** `GeneralContractor` / `HomeAndConstructionBusiness` (sub-type of `LocalBusiness`), with `Service` and area-served markup on service-area pages.
- **Verdict:** **Adapt the existing `/templates/tradies/` template — no new template required.** Add a masonry-grade before/after gallery and a builder-reference block; tune palette to masonry tones. This is a content/section variation of the tradies base, not a new build.

## Phase 6 — Niche-specific upsells

- **SEO module:** **medium.** Local "bricklayer near me / [suburb]" search demand exists but is modest, and much of the niche's work is builder-referral rather than search-driven, capping SEO upside. Worth offering, not the headline.
- **Local Services Ads (LSA):** **likely not available — flag to verify.** Google's LSA vertical eligibility in Australia is limited and changes; bricklaying is not a confirmed eligible vertical. **Do not promise LSA without checking Google's current AU eligibility list.**
- **Review acquisition:** **high.** Masonry is trust- and craftsmanship-driven; before/after plus strong Google reviews materially lift conversion. This is the strongest upsell fit.
- **Niche-specific upsells:**
  - **Quote calculator** (per m² / per 1,000 bricks) — fits the quote-led cycle.
  - **Portfolio/gallery management** (owners add job photos from the phone).
  - **Builder-referral landing pages** (capture the channel that actually drives work).

## Phase 7 — Unit economics

*All inputs flagged as estimates; validate against live cohort data.*

**CAC** = cost per free-preview signup ÷ free→paid conversion rate. Behaviour-only targeting → signup CPL toward the mid/high AU range.

| Scenario | Signup CPL | Free→paid conv. | **CAC** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bear | $40 | 6% | **~$667** |
| Base | $25 | 10% | **~$250** |
| Bull | $15 | 15% | **~$100** |

**LTV** = entry $39/mo × projected tenure (blended slightly up for partial upgrades).

| Scenario | Tenure | Notes | **LTV** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bear | 8 mo | high churn, low-sophistication owner | **~$312** |
| Base | 15 mo | entry-heavy, light upgrade | **~$585–$650** |
| Bull | 24+ mo | upgrades to growth tier | **~$1,200+** |

**LTV:CAC** — Bear **~0.5:1 (fails)** · Base **~2.3–2.6:1 (marginal — below 3:1 target, above 2:1 floor)** · Bull **~12:1.**

**Payback period:** base CAC $250 ÷ $39 ≈ **6.4 months.**

**Read:** base-case economics are **marginal, not strong.** The drag comes from (a) behaviour-only Meta targeting → higher CAC, (b) builder-referral demand → a lead-gen website captures less of the niche's total work, and (c) price-sensitivity in a flat industry → low entry price and churn risk. The niche is viable but does not clear the 3:1 bar in the base case.

## Phase 8 — Competitive landscape

- **Niche/tradie-specific builders:** a crowded AU field of tradie website providers — **heytradie, busi.au, Tradiecard, Websites Factory, ServiceScale, BuiltByJack** and similar, typically **~$99/month managed**, template-based, generic across trades.
- **Generic builders:** **Wix / Squarespace** ($30–$50/mo) with DIY effort; **Durable** (AI builder) entering the AU SMB space.
- **Local agencies:** numerous AU agencies doing **$1,500–$5,000+** one-off tradie sites.
- **Pricing benchmark:** managed ~$99/mo is the number to beat; DIY $30–50/mo is the floor.
- **Differentiating angle for Preview Factory:** **AI-generated, niche-true preview live in ~3 hours, free trial, with programmatic local-SEO service-area pages and call tracking** framed as *"more local brick & block jobs"* — at **$39 entry, well under the $99 managed pack.** The differentiation is *speed-to-see-it* + *local-SEO depth* + *price*, not generic "we build websites."

## Phase 9 — Decision

**Decision: HOLD.**

Bricklaying passes the screen and is cheap to support (it adapts the existing tradies template, no new build). But the base-case unit economics are **marginal (~2.3–2.6:1 LTV:CAC, below the 3:1 target)**, and three structural factors weaken it relative to other trades: demand is heavily **builder-referral** (limiting the value of a consumer lead-gen site), Meta offers **no usable interest** (behaviour-only targeting raises CAC), and the industry is **flat/declining** with price-sensitive owners. Bricklaying is better captured as **one trade within a broader multi-trade "tradies" offering** than launched as a standalone v1 niche. Revisit if (a) a tradies customer-list lookalike drives CAC toward the bull case, or (b) builder-referral landing pages prove they convert.

## Phase 10 — Implementation priority

- **Priority tier:** **Tier 3** (future expansion, months 7+) as a standalone niche — or, preferably, **fold into a general "tradies" template family** marketed across multiple trades rather than a dedicated bricklayer launch.
- **Build requirements:** none new. Adapt `/templates/tradies/`: masonry palette, before/after gallery, builder-reference block, optional per-m²/per-1,000-brick quote calculator. Schema `GeneralContractor`/`HomeAndConstructionBusiness`.
- **Compliance considerations:** standard Meta ad policy; no Special Ad Category triggers (B2B website offer). Ensure trade-licence/insurance claims on generated sites are owner-supplied, not fabricated.
