## Phase 1 — Market sizing

- **Estimated business count:** the niche splits across two IBISWorld industries that we treat as one go-to-market segment:
  - **Residential Cleaning Services:** ~**17,676** businesses (IBISWorld, 2025), growing at a **~8.3% CAGR (2020–25)** in business count — one of the fastest-growing small-service business populations we have measured.
  - **Commercial Cleaning Services:** ~**44,775** businesses (IBISWorld, 2025), ~**4.7% CAGR (2020–25)** in business count.
  - **Combined addressable population ≈ 60,000+** cleaning businesses nationally — materially larger than electrician (~45,850) and one of the largest niches in the programme. *(Note: some operators do both domestic and commercial and may be double-counted across the two industries — treat 60k as an upper-bound triangulation, with a conservative working figure of ~50,000 distinct businesses.)*
- **Industry revenue:** **Residential ~$1.4bn** (IBISWorld, ~2.6% CAGR 2020–25); **Commercial ~$20.2bn** market size (IBISWorld 2025, +1.9% YoY, ~5.9% CAGR 2020–25). The commercial segment is an order of magnitude larger by revenue but more contract/B2B-driven; residential is the higher-fit segment for a consumer-facing lead-gen site.
- **Geographic distribution:** no clean per-niche ABS split available; cleaning demand tracks **population, household density and commercial floorspace**, concentrating in **Greater Sydney (NSW), Melbourne (VIC), South-East Queensland (Brisbane/Gold Coast/Sunshine Coast)**, then **Perth (WA)** and **Adelaide (SA)**. Demand is genuinely everywhere there are homes and offices — not confined to growth corridors. *(Triangulation only — confirm with ABS by SA4 before targeting spend.)*
- **Growth rate estimate:** **strong and structural.** Residential business count growing ~8.3%/yr reflects (a) low barriers to entry (sole traders, low capital), (b) rising demand for outsourced domestic cleaning (dual-income households, ageing population, NDIS-funded domestic assistance), and (c) the large, recurring end-of-lease/bond-clean market driven by the rental churn cycle. The high entry rate is double-edged: a large, continuously-renewing pool of **new** operators who need a website (excellent for our "just started" angle), but also high churn at the bottom of the market.
- **Addressable subset (Meta-reachable):** against ~50,000+ businesses, most are sole traders / micro-crews with a findable digital footprint (FB Page, recent business admin, GBP). Realistic reachable owner-operator pool is a **working estimate of 25,000–40,000 nationally** — the **largest addressable pool of any niche analysed so far**, and reachable via both interest *and* behaviour (see Phase 4). *(Estimate — unverified.)*

## Phase 2 — Customer profile

- **Annual business revenue range:** **sole-trader residential cleaners ~$50k–$120k** revenue (industry/operator data; ~$30–$50/hr typical rate), take-home **$50k–$75k**. Small crews (2–5) and commercial operators materially higher (commercial contracts scale into six and seven figures). Lower revenue base than electrician (~$282k) → **entry pricing must be a clear no-brainer**; this is a more price-sensitive owner.
- **Team size:** **sole trader or small crew** dominant in residential; commercial skews to small-team-to-established. The owner is on the tools and also doing quoting/scheduling/"marketing."
- **Decision-maker:** the **owner**, very frequently with a **spouse/partner** handling bookings, phone and online presence. This is one of the most common "couple-run" niches — the spouse is often the de facto digital decision-maker.
- **Current digital sophistication:** **low.** Predominantly **Facebook-only or unmanaged GBP**, frequently **no website at all** or a free one-page builder site. Lower digital sophistication than electricians (fewer use job-management apps). This is a **textbook fit** for "you have no real website / your site doesn't match how good your cleaning is."
- **Average customer transaction value:** wide and importantly **recurring**:
  - **Regular domestic clean (weekly/fortnightly):** **~$120–$300 per visit** (commonly $50–$65/hr × 2–4 hrs).
  - **One-off / spring / deep clean:** **$200–$500**.
  - **End-of-lease / bond clean:** **$290–$750+** (studio ~$220–$270; large 4-bed/2-bath $700+) — high-value, high-intent, high-search-volume.
  - **Commercial contracts:** recurring monthly, $500–$5,000+/mo.
- **Customer lifetime value to the business:** **this is the niche's headline strength.** A **recurring weekly/fortnightly domestic client** at ~$150/visit, fortnightly, retained 1–3 years = **$3,900–$11,700+** LTV from a single customer. A commercial cleaning contract is **$6k–$60k+/yr** recurring. Even the transactional bond-clean segment refers and repeats. **Recurring revenue makes cleaning a genuinely high-LTV niche to its operators**, far above the $300 screen — and the link "more enquiries → recurring contracts" is exactly the value frame our product sells.
- **Typical marketing budget per month:** **$100–$800**, lower and less planned than electricians. Many spend ~$0 (word-of-mouth + Facebook groups + Airtasker/Oneflare lead fees). Lead-marketplace fees (Airtasker/Oneflare/hipages) are a real, resented current spend we can anchor against.
- **Booking/inquiry cycle:** **mixed — scheduled/quoted (regular cleans, commercial) AND fast-turnaround (bond cleans before an inspection deadline, one-off deep cleans).** The bond-clean segment is **deadline-driven and high-intent** — "book my end-of-lease clean this week" — which makes "more enquiries now" a visceral value frame.

## Phase 3 — Willingness to pay

**Reference points (AUD):**

- **Managed tradie/SMB website services:** ~**$99/month** (heytradie, busi.au and similar managed providers cover cleaning as a vertical), typically no/low setup.
- **DIY builders (Wix / Squarespace):** **$30–$50/month** + many hours of owner time.
- **Agency one-off build:** **$3,000–$10,000** typical AU SMB site; **managed maintenance $169–$400+/mo**.
- **Existing spend:** many cleaners pay **lead-marketplace fees** (Airtasker/Oneflare/hipages) per lead and spend $100–$800/mo on marketing — a fixed website subscription is small and more predictable than per-lead fees.
- **Value of one customer:** a **single new recurring fortnightly client** ($150 × 26/yr ≈ **$3,900/yr**, often multi-year) returns **many years** of any tier below in its first month of retention. One bond clean ($290–$750) covers months of the entry tier. ROI is overwhelmingly favourable; the constraint is the owner's **price sensitivity and perceived value**, not the maths.

**Recommended tiers (AUD/month):**

| Tier | Price | Justification |
|---|---|---|
| **Entry** | **$39** | Set **below** electrician's $49 because the cleaning owner has a **lower revenue base ($50–120k vs $282k), lower digital sophistication and higher price sensitivity** — the no-brainer bar is lower. Still signals "managed-grade, not a toy" (above the $30 DIY floor) and undercuts the ~$99 managed benchmark by ~60%. One recurring fortnightly client retained one month returns ~8× the annual entry cost — an overwhelming no-brainer. |
| **Growth** | **$89** | Just under the ~$99 managed-competitor headline while delivering **more** (programmatic local service-area pages + review acquisition + bond-clean / end-of-lease landing pages + instant-quote form). The "same value, less than the managed pack" anchor. Targets owners actively chasing recurring residential and bond-clean work. |
| **Scale** | **$179** | Adds Google/Meta ads management + online booking/quote tooling for owners pursuing recurring-contract and commercial volume, where a single managed lead converts to a multi-thousand-dollar recurring contract. **Note: LSA management — a Scale-tier driver for electricians — is NOT yet available for cleaning in Australia (see Phase 6), so the Scale tier leans on Google Ads + booking automation instead.** Priced below electrician's $199 to reflect the absent LSA hook and lower revenue base. |

*Flagged uncertainty: tier prices are reasoned against market anchors, not tested. The strong recurring-LTV argument supports holding firm on price; validate with a live price test before locking.*

## Phase 4 — Meta ads targeting viability

Verified against `strategy/research/meta-job-titles.md` (**Category 11 — Home services (non-trade)**, flagged as a strong product-fit group).

- **Job-title / interest availability:** **"Cleaning / House cleaning / Commercial cleaning" is a 🟢 reliable topic interest** — but, per the master file, it is **heavily consumer-contaminated** (mixes homeowners/tenants wanting a cleaner with operators) and franchise-brand-following. **"End-of-lease / Bond cleaning" is 🔴 (AU term, thin); "Carpet cleaning" is 🟡.** So the interest is a **narrowing/lookalike-seed signal, not the spine.**
- **Behavioural targeting (the spine):** 🟢 reliable platform behaviours, ideally suited to this niche given the high rate of new entrants —
  - **Small business owners** (primary)
  - **New active business admins (<12 / <24 months)** — exceptionally well-matched here, since residential cleaning has a **~8.3%/yr new-business formation rate**: a continuously-renewing pool of "just started, needs a site" operators.
  - **Facebook Page admins** — proxy for "runs a business with a FB presence, weak/no website" = our ICP exactly (cleaners are FB-page-heavy, website-light).
- **Recommended stack:** *Small business owners* / *New active business admins* **+ "Cleaning/House cleaning" interest as a narrowing layer + tight suburb/postcode geo**, graduating to **AU 1–3% lookalikes** from converters once seed volume exists. The "new business admins" angle is stronger here than in almost any other niche.
- **Geographic targeting:** broad metro + regional **suburb/postcode clusters** — demand is everywhere there are households and offices. Suburb-level where audience size allows; bond-clean demand concentrates around high-rental-churn suburbs.
- **Estimated CPM:** AU baseline ~**$11** (2026 range ~$10.68–$14.34; AU runs ~21% below global). *(Reused from the 2026 AU Meta benchmark; re-verify.)*
- **Estimated CTR / CPL:** home-services local lead creative reaches **~$8–$12 CPL** at the efficient end; broader AU range **$20–$80**. Cleaning benefits from **interest + behaviour layering, a very large addressable pool, and a strong creative hook (recurring clients / bond-clean urgency)** → expect the **mid-to-efficient** end. The large new-entrant pool should keep audiences fresh and CPMs reasonable.
- **Creative angle:** *"Get more regular cleaning clients in [suburb] — see your new website free in 3 hours,"* with a secondary bond-clean/end-of-lease urgency hook for the transactional segment.

**Verdict:** `meta_targeting_viable: true` (interest + behaviour). One of the **strongest targeting profiles in the programme**: a usable 🟢 interest, the full behavioural spine, an unusually strong *New active business admins* fit (high new-entrant rate), the largest addressable pool analysed, and a credible recurring-revenue creative hook → low expected CAC.

## Phase 5 — Category mapping & tuning

**Category mapping:** **`trades`.** "Home services (non-trade)" is **not one of the seven visual categories** — so it must map to the closest fit. Cleaning maps cleanly to **`trades`**: it shares the **service-business visual language** (local owner-operator serving homes/businesses across a service area), the **same site architecture** (instant-quote/booking CTA, services grid, service-area pages, reviews/trust, FAQ, about), the same **"book a local service provider"** conversion model, and the same trust-signal pattern (insurance, guarantees, local proof). The layout, section set and component needs are **substantially identical to a plumber/electrician site** — there is no structural reason to build a new template. **No `category_required: new` flag.** *(Honest assessment: the only argument for `new` would be a softer, more domestic/lifestyle aesthetic than hard trades — but that is a **tuning** difference within the category palette, not a new layout system. Handle it via generation-time tuning, below.)*

**Niche-specific tuning within the `trades` category:**

- **Colour emphasis:** within the trades palette, lean **fresh, clean, hygienic** — a bright **teal/aqua or clean blue primary** with **white/light neutral dominance** and a soft green accent (the universal "clean/fresh" cue), distinctly *lighter and softer* than electrician's safety-blue/amber or bricklaying's earthen masonry tones. Airy, spacious, spotless feel — the page itself should signal cleanliness.
- **Critical sections (prominence order):**
  1. **Instant quote / online booking CTA** with click-to-call — top priority above the fold; cleaning buyers want a fast price/booking, and the bond-clean segment is deadline-driven.
  2. **Services + frequency grid** — regular (weekly/fortnightly/monthly), one-off/deep/spring, **end-of-lease / bond clean** (call it out prominently — high-value, high-intent), move-in/move-out, commercial/office. Frequency options are a defining feature of this niche.
  3. **Service-area pages** (programmatic local SEO — "cleaner [suburb]", "bond cleaning [suburb]" carry strong continuous search demand).
  4. **Reviews / Google rating + trust block** — cleaning is a *trust-into-your-home* purchase; social proof is unusually load-bearing.
  5. **Bond-clean / end-of-lease landing pages** with a **"100% bond-back / re-clean guarantee"** hook — a major conversion driver for the transactional segment.
  6. **Quote / booking CTA** repeat + call tracking.
- **Trust signal priorities (niche-specific):** **(1) Police / background checks** (cleaners enter homes — this is the #1 differentiating trust signal for domestic cleaning and should be displayed prominently), **(2) public liability insurance**, **(3) satisfaction / bond-back guarantee**, (4) "vetted, trained, English-speaking staff," (5) years of experience and Google rating, (6) eco/non-toxic products (a real preference driver for families/pets). Note: no occupational *licence* exists for general cleaning (unlike electrical), so **police checks + insurance + guarantee replace the licence number** as the headline trust trio.
- **Content tone:** **warm, reassuring, trustworthy** — softer and more domestic than the safety-authoritative electrician voice. "Trusted, vetted, your home in good hands, satisfaction guaranteed." For the bond-clean segment, shift to **direct/urgent** ("get your full bond back — guaranteed re-clean").
- **Image style cues (stock fallback):** bright sunlit spotless interiors, a friendly uniformed cleaner (smiling, approachable, not industrial), sparkling kitchens/bathrooms, before/after of a deep clean, eco-friendly product cues. Light, airy, fresh — avoid clinical/industrial or dark imagery.
- **Schema.org subtype:** **`HouseCleaningService`** (a defined subtype of `LocalBusiness` → `ProfessionalService`) for domestic operators; use **`CleaningService`** patterning for mixed/commercial. Add `Service` and `areaServed` markup on service-area pages, and `AggregateRating` on the reviews block.

**Build dependency:** the `trades` category currently exists only as a **single-page** component (`/templates/tradies/page.tsx`, with `index.tsx` re-exporting it) — there is **no multi-page site system** and **no `/templates/categories/`** directory yet (no `SiteProps`). House cleaning needs the same **multi-page trades site system** every niche now requires. **Flag: trades category must be expanded to the multi-page architecture (Phase C) before this niche can be generated as specified.** This is the **same category-level dependency flagged for electrician** — not a cleaning-specific build. Cleaning then rides the trades multi-page system with generation-time tuning per the above.

## Phase 6 — Niche-specific upsells

- **SEO module:** **high.** "Cleaner near me / [suburb] house cleaning / **bond cleaning [suburb]** / end of lease cleaning / commercial cleaning [suburb]" carry **strong, continuous local search volume** with direct purchase intent — and bond-clean is a deadline-driven, high-conversion query. Programmatic service-area + service-type pages have real upside. Headline upsell.
- **Local Services Ads (LSA):** **NOT a current upsell — LSA is not yet generally available in Australia.** House Cleaning **is** an eligible LSA *vertical* in markets where LSA runs (US/UK/CA etc.), but multiple 2025 sources indicate **LSA is not yet live for Australian advertisers** (only "select areas," with community guidance that it is effectively unavailable). **This is the key contrast with electrician** (whose LSA hook was confirmed). Treat LSA as a **future upside**, not a launch upsell. *(Re-verify quarterly — if/when Google opens LSA in AU, cleaning's eligibility makes it an immediate Scale-tier addition.)*
- **Review acquisition:** **very high — arguably the single most valuable upsell here.** Cleaning is a trust-into-the-home purchase; Google rating directly drives both organic conversion and the trust block. Strongest review-acquisition fit of the niches analysed.
- **Niche-specific upsells:**
  - **Online booking + instant quote calculator** (by bedrooms/bathrooms/frequency) — extremely high fit; reduces phone friction and captures deadline-driven bond-clean bookings.
  - **Recurring-booking / subscription scheduling** — monetises and reinforces the recurring-revenue model that is this niche's economic core.
  - **Bond-clean / end-of-lease landing-page pack** with bond-back-guarantee messaging (high-value transactional segment).
  - **Google Ads management** (steps into the gap left by LSA's absence in AU).

## Phase 7 — Unit economics

*All inputs flagged as estimates; validate against live cohort data.*

**CAC** = cost per free-preview signup ÷ free→paid conversion. Interest + behaviour targeting, the largest addressable pool, a strong *New active business admins* fit and a recurring-client creative hook → signup CPL toward the **mid/efficient** AU range.

| Scenario | Signup CPL | Free→paid conv. | **CAC** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bear | $35 | 6% | **~$583** |
| Base | $20 | 10% | **~$200** |
| Bull | $11 | 15% | **~$73** |

**LTV** = entry $39/mo × projected tenure, blended up for upgrades. **Recurring-revenue economics on the customer's side support longer tenure** — once a cleaner's site is generating recurring clients, the cost of switching/cancelling is high (the site *is* their booking funnel), supporting retention above a transactional trade.

| Scenario | Tenure | Notes | **LTV** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bear | 9 mo | high churn, price-sensitive new-entrant sole trader who folds | **~$351** |
| Base | 20 mo | entry→partial upgrade, blended ARPU ~$50; recurring-client value frame aids retention | **~$1,000** |
| Bull | 30+ mo | upgrades to growth/scale (ads + booking), embedded as booking funnel | **~$2,200+** |

**LTV:CAC** — Bear **~0.6:1 (fails)** · Base **~5:1 (clears the 3:1 target comfortably)** · Bull **~30:1.**

**Payback period:** base CAC $200 ÷ ~$50 blended ARPU ≈ **4.0 months.**

**Read:** base-case economics are **strong and clear the 3:1 bar (~5:1)**, driven by (a) the **largest addressable pool** of any niche (low CAC potential), (b) interest + behaviour + strong new-admin targeting, (c) **recurring-revenue economics** that make the value frame believable and aid retention, and (d) very high review-acquisition and booking-tool upsell fit. The base case actually edges out electrician on LTV:CAC despite a lower entry price, because the cleaning pool is larger/cheaper to reach and tenure benefits from the embedded-booking-funnel effect. The bear case still fails on the **high new-entrant churn** characteristic of this market (lots of operators start and fold within a year) — so **activation, demonstrated enquiry volume, and onboarding stickiness are the critical retention levers.**

## Phase 8 — Competitive landscape

- **Niche/managed builders:** the AU managed-SMB-site field (**heytradie, busi.au, Tradiecard, Websites Factory, ServiceScale**) covers cleaning as a vertical at **~$99/month**, template-based and generic. There is **no dominant cleaning-specific website product** — cleaning is treated as a generic "home service," leaving room for a cleaning-true offer.
- **Lead marketplaces (the real incumbent competitor):** **Airtasker, Oneflare, hipages, Service.com.au** dominate how cleaners currently get online leads — **per-lead fees**, no owned web presence, no SEO equity. Our angle directly attacks this: *"stop renting leads — own a site that brings you recurring clients."*
- **Generic builders:** **Wix / Squarespace** ($30–$50/mo, DIY); **Durable** (AI builder) entering AU SMB.
- **Local agencies:** numerous AU agencies doing **$3,000–$10,000** one-off cleaning sites + monthly SEO/Google Ads; specialist cleaning-marketing agencies (e.g. cleaning-focused Google Ads shops) validate the ads upsell market.
- **Pricing benchmark:** managed ~$99/mo to beat; DIY $30–50/mo floor; agency $3k–10k one-off + $169–400+/mo maintenance.
- **Differentiating angle for Preview Factory:** **AI-generated, cleaning-true preview live in ~3 hours, free trial, with programmatic local-SEO service-area + bond-clean pages, instant-quote/booking, police-check/insurance/bond-back trust framing, recurring-booking tooling and call tracking** — framed as *"more regular cleaning clients in your suburb, on a site you own instead of leads you rent"* — at **$39 entry, well under the $99 managed pack and cheaper than chronic Airtasker/Oneflare lead fees.** Differentiation = *speed-to-see-it* + *own-vs-rent leads* + *recurring-client framing* + *price*.

## Phase 9 — Decision

**Decision: GO.**

House cleaning is a flagship home-services niche for v1 and one of the strongest overall fits in the programme. It clears the screen comfortably and clears the **3:1 LTV:CAC bar in the base case (~5:1, ~4-month payback)** — edging out electrician despite a lower entry price. Four structural strengths drive this: the **largest addressable business pool analysed (~50,000+, growing ~8.3%/yr in residential)** with low CAC potential; **strong, low-cost Meta targeting** (a 🟢 interest plus the full behavioural spine, with an unusually strong *New active business admins* fit given the high new-entrant rate); **recurring weekly/fortnightly revenue** that makes the "more local clients" value frame believable and supports tenure as the site becomes the owner's booking funnel; and **very high review-acquisition and online-booking upsell fit**. Willingness-to-pay is lower than electrician (revenue base $50–120k, price-sensitive owners), so the entry tier is set at **$39** to keep it an overwhelming no-brainer against the recurring-client maths. The two notable contrasts with electrician are **(a) LSA is not yet available in Australia for cleaning**, removing a Scale-tier hook (mitigated by Google Ads + booking tooling, and a future upside if Google opens AU LSA), and **(b) high new-entrant churn** at the bottom of the market, which sinks the bear case and makes activation/onboarding the key retention levers. The only build blocker is the **category-level dependency**: the `trades` template must be expanded from single-page to the multi-page site system before this niche can be generated as specified — the same dependency already flagged for electrician.

## Phase 10 — Implementation priority

- **Priority tier:** **Tier 1** (launch in first 90 days) — flagship home-services niche, largest addressable pool, strongest base-case economics, high search demand and recurring-revenue retention story.
- **Build requirements:** **category-level, not cleaning-specific.** Expand the `trades` category from the current single-page `/templates/tradies/page.tsx` (re-exported via `index.tsx`) to the **multi-page site system** (home + services + service-detail + location/service-area + bond-clean landing + FAQ + about) under the new `SiteProps` schema (Phase C / Phase D). - **Compliance considerations:** standard Meta ad policy; **no Special Ad Category triggers** (B2B website offer). Ensure **police-check, insurance and guarantee claims on generated sites are owner-supplied, not fabricated** (trust-into-the-home niche — false safety claims are a real liability). If NDIS-funded domestic-assistance providers are targeted, note NDIS provider-claim sensitivities. Re-verify **AU LSA availability quarterly** — if Google opens it, cleaning's existing LSA eligibility makes it an immediate Scale-tier upsell addition.
