Everything not listed here passes the screen and proceeds to the ten-phase methodology in the prescribed order (trades → mobile services → allied health → beauty/aesthetics → everything else; alphabetical within each).

## Decisions

**Electrician / Electrical Services — GO (2026-06-19).** First niche analysed under the revised methodology (Phase 5 = category mapping & tuning); serves as the A3 calibration run, deliberately chosen as a clear-GO trade to contrast bricklaying's HOLD. Australia has ~45,850 electrical-services businesses (IBISWorld, 2025) in a ~$36.1bn industry — down ~3% YoY on the construction cycle but with a strong structural tailwind: Jobs and Skills Australia flags a persistent electrician shortage and a need for 53,000–84,000 more electricians for the net-zero transition (solar, batteries, EV chargers, electrification). Sole-trader revenue averages ~$282,520 with owner take-home $80k–100k+, supporting recommended tiers $49/$99/$199 (entry undercuts the ~$99 managed benchmark by ~50% while signalling managed-grade; Scale adds ads/LSA management). Unlike bricklaying, demand is heavily direct-to-end-customer (search + emergency fault calls), so a lead-gen site captures attributable jobs; Meta targeting is stronger (a usable 🟢 "Electrician" interest layered on the behavioural spine, vs bricklaying's behaviour-only); and electricians are a confirmed Google LSA-eligible vertical, a differentiated Scale-tier upsell. Base-case unit economics clear the bar (~4.5:1 LTV:CAC, ~4-month payback); the bear case fails on sole-trader churn, so activation and demonstrated call volume are the retention levers. Maps to the `trades` visual category — no new template — but carries a category-level build dependency: the trades template is currently single-page and must be expanded to the multi-page site system (Phase C) before generation. Priority: Tier 1 (first 90 days).

**Plumbing / Plumbing Services — GO (2026-06-19).** Plumbing is a flagship v1 trade alongside electrician: ~28,615 AU businesses (IBISWorld 2025, ~$22.2bn industry), direct-to-homeowner demand, and the highest emergency-call share of any portfolio trade, which makes the "more phone calls now" frame the most visceral we have. It clears the 3:1 bar in the base case (~4.5:1 LTV:CAC, ~4-month payback; bear fails on sole-trader churn), with usable Meta targeting (🟢 Plumber/Plumbing interest + Small-business-owner/Page-admin behavioural spine + suburb geo) and a $49/$99/$199 tier stack anchored against the ~$99 managed-tradie benchmark. Two differentiators stand out — confirmed Australian LSA (Google Guaranteed) eligibility for a Scale-tier upsell, and an "own your leads instead of renting them from hipages/Airtasker" wedge against lead marketplaces. The sole blocker is the shared trades build dependency: the category exists only as single-page `templates/tradies/page.tsx` and must be expanded to the multi-page site system before generation. Assigned Tier 1.

**House Cleaning / Cleaning Services — GO (2026-06-19).** House cleaning is a flagship home-services niche and one of the strongest overall fits in the programme, with the largest addressable pool analysed (~17,676 residential businesses growing ~8.3%/yr plus ~44,775 commercial; ~50,000 distinct operators) and base-case economics of ~5:1 LTV:CAC at ~4-month payback that edge out electrician despite a lower $39 entry price. The economics are powered by recurring weekly/fortnightly client revenue (a single retained fortnightly client is worth ~$3,900/yr+), which makes the "more local clients" value frame believable and lifts tenure as the site becomes the owner's booking funnel. It maps to the `trades` visual category (no new template — only generation-time tuning: clean teal/aqua palette, instant-quote/booking and services-by-frequency prominence, bond-clean landing pages, and a police-check/insurance/bond-back trust trio replacing the trade-licence signal), and Meta targeting is excellent via Category 11 (interest + behavioural spine, with an unusually strong New-active-business-admins fit given the high new-entrant rate). Two contrasts with electrician temper it: Google LSA is not yet available in Australia, removing the LSA Scale-tier hook (Google Ads + booking automation substitute; re-verify quarterly), and high new-entrant churn sinks the bear case, making activation and demonstrated enquiry volume the critical retention levers. Tier 1, blocked only by the same trades multi-page build dependency as electrician.

**Physiotherapy — GO (2026-06-19).** Physiotherapy is the calibration niche for the allied-health category and clears the 3:1 bar in the base case (~4.9:1 LTV:CAC, ~4.1-month payback) off a structurally growing market (~9,497 clinics, +5.2% YoY; ~$3.9bn industry, ~$410k avg revenue/clinic) with direct-to-patient search demand and higher willingness-to-pay than trades — supporting a higher $69/$129/$249 tier ladder that undercuts the ~$495/mo allied-health managed benchmark. It is more CAC-sensitive than electrician (smaller owner pool, patient-contaminated Meta interest, no health-condition targeting, higher health CPMs), so the bear case fails on high CPL plus churn, making clinic-owner lookalikes, a "convert/rank/compliant" creative angle (not "you have no site"), and demonstrated booking volume the decisive levers. Two niche complications: higher digital sophistication/incumbency (most clinics already have a site) and the Ahpra advertising overlay (no clinical-outcome testimonials, no cure/guarantee claims, owner-supplied credentials), which must be baked into the schema, generation prompt and review tool. The principal blocker is a category-level build dependency: the allied-health template is currently a one-line stub with no multi-page system, so the entire allied-health site system must be built from scratch before this niche can ship. Tier 1.

**Hairdressing / Hair Salon — GO (2026-06-19).** Maps to the `beauty-aesthetics` visual category, which the Meta master file flags as the single best-fit category for our model — a relatively un-diluted 🟢 "Hairdresser/Hair salon" interest plus the behavioural spine plus an Instagram-native, visually-motivated owner whose biggest weakness is precisely the owned website we sell. Base-case unit economics clear the bar (~3.5:1 LTV:CAC, ~5.2-month payback) but with less headroom than electrician: mid-margin price-sensitivity forces a lower $39 entry and ~$48 blended ARPU, the beauty-category CPM runs hot (~$12.46), and there is no LSA upsell to lift the Scale tier. The differentiating wedges are visual quality (what this owner cares about most), built-in online booking, and "own your bookings, no commission" against Fresha. The principal blocker is the largest build dependency seen so far — the `beauty-aesthetics` template is currently a single-line stub and must be built from scratch as a multi-page site system with first-class booking and gallery components before this niche can ship; Tier 1, sequenced after the trades build.

**Personal Training / Fitness Studio — GO (2026-06-19).** A Meta-native, highly visual `fitness-wellness` niche spanning sole-trader PTs, small-group studios and the booming reformer-Pilates segment (~8.4% CAGR), set against a flat overall gym industry (~$3.7bn, -1.3% YoY). The GO is studio-led: base-case unit economics clear the bar only narrowly (LTV:CAC ~3.2:1, ~5-month payback) and the bear case fails hard (~0.5:1), carried by recurring membership LTV and studios' existing ads budgets rather than margin-thin sole traders. Two risks must be managed actively — audience inversion (Meta's giant "fitness/gym/pilates" interests reach clients, not owners, so a behavioural spine + early lookalikes + owner-qualifying B2B creative are non-negotiable) and sole-trader fragility ("Instagram is enough"). Pricing is set defensively at $39/$89/$179 (entry below the ~$89 Mindbody floor; growth anchored to it; no LSA upsell, so scale caps below electrician). The decisive blocker is a net-new category build: `fitness-wellness` does not exist at all, requiring a full multi-page site system including booking-embed and Instagram-feed components before launch. Tier 1, conditional on that build landing.

**Café / Coffee Shop — HOLD (2026-06-19).** A large (~15,251 businesses, +7.6% YoY), highly reachable, visually-native niche that maps cleanly to the `hospitality` visual category — but the economics don't clear the bar in the base case (LTV:CAC ~1.1:1 against a 2:1 floor, ~13-month payback). Three structural forces drive it: a $25 entry anchored at the low-margin hospitality floor (3–6% net margins, no entrenched site-subscription habit) drags LTV; owner-isolation is the hardest of any Meta category (café interest is ~all consumers, behavioural layer mandatory) and the offer is low-urgency, lifting CAC; and the upsell ladder is short (no LSA, reviews overlaps free GBP, ordering is bought on commission). Deeper than the numbers, there is a genuine product-frame mismatch — Preview Factory's retention engine is tracked phone calls, but cafés win by walk-ins, Maps, reviews and Instagram, so the proof-of-value loop is absent, while cheap Squarespace plus free social/GBP inertia compete hard for exactly this design-literate owner. Held rather than skipped because the bull profile (strong-brand, retail-coffee, multi-venue operators) clears comfortably (~7:1) and this is the lead use-case for the not-yet-built hospitality category; revisit for GO only if a live owner-targeted CPL test lands near the bull end and the review/ordering retention loop proves out. Tier 3, with a heavy greenfield hospitality build dependency.

**Accounting / Bookkeeping — HOLD (2026-06-19).** Maps to the `professional-services` visual category; ~36,717 accounting-services businesses (IBISWorld 2025, +0.5%) plus an independent-bookkeeping tail give a combined working estimate of ~45,000–55,000 practices, with very high recurring client LTV ($5k–$30k+) and a reachable Meta B2B behavioural spine (Business decision-makers + Small business owners + Accounting interest). It nonetheless fails the product-fit test the master file flagged in advance: high baseline digital sophistication means a large share already has a competent, lead-capable site and genuinely doesn't need us, while referral-led, consultation-cycle, partly-remote demand mutes the core "more local phone calls now" outcome frame and forces colder, harder creative against a numerate, skeptical buyer. Acquisition economics are weaker than trades (higher professional-services CPM, ~0.78% B2B CTR, base CAC ~$475 ≈ 2× electrician, ~5-month payback, and a bear case that fails at 0.7:1 once the needs-us minority is exhausted), so the ~4:1 base LTV:CAC is real but thin and fragile at scale. Recommended tiers $79/$149/$299 (above trades for credibility/signalling, not affordability), with no LSA upsell. Largest build dependency of any niche assessed — the `professional-services` category is a net-new multi-page system — so HOLD as a Tier-3 revisit once the category is built and validated on a better-fit professional niche and a customer-list lookalike de-risks CAC.

**Florist / Flower Shop — HOLD (2026-06-19).** Florist maps cleanly to the `retail-boutique` visual category and passes the surface screen (owner-operated, GBP presence, and the standout owner-adjacent 🟢 Meta interest in the entire Retail category), but it is held rather than GO'd because the dominant florist conversion is e-commerce / online checkout, not a phone lead — the Online Flower Shops segment alone is ~$495m and growing faster than shopfronts, the market is already served by transactional platforms (Flower Store in a Box, FloristWare, Shopify with checkout at $29–79), and many florists already have a transacting site, making the product's non-transactional "more local calls" frame a poor fit outside the narrow weddings/events enquiry slice. Base-case economics are marginal (LTV:CAC ~2.3:1 — clears the 2:1 floor, misses the 3:1 target), dragged down by a small addressable owner pool (~2,000–3,000), lower margins and higher churn (Shopify is a one-click escape), no LSA eligibility, and a thinner high-end upsell. It also carries a heavy build dependency — the `retail-boutique` category does not exist in `/templates/` and would need building from scratch. Reassess as a conditional Tier-3 candidate after a stronger retail niche justifies the category build, ideally scoped to weddings/events-only or once the product gains a checkout/commerce capability.

**Bricklaying / Blockwork — HOLD (2026-06-19).** Australia has ~9,297 bricklaying businesses (IBISWorld) in a flat-to-declining ~$3.5–4.0bn industry; operators are mostly sole traders/small crews ($100k–$250k revenue) with low digital sophistication and work that comes largely via builder referral and word of mouth rather than consumer search. Customer LTV ($2k–$20k jobs; recurring builder relationships) clears the screen comfortably. Recommended tiers $39/$79/$149 undercut the ~$99 managed-tradie benchmark to be a no-brainer. Meta targeting is behaviour-only — no usable "bricklayer" interest exists, so reach depends on Small business owners / new active business admins / Page admins + suburb geo + lookalikes, raising CAC. Base-case unit economics are marginal (~2.3–2.6:1 LTV:CAC, below the 3:1 target; ~6.4-month payback). The niche adapts the existing tradies template with no new build, so it is cheap to support, but the combination of flat demand, builder-referral channel and imprecise targeting makes it a Tier 3 / fold-into-multi-trade candidate rather than a standalone v1 launch.
